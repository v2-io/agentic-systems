## Research Report: "A Mathematical Theory of Agency and Intelligence" (arXiv 2602.22519) -- Authors, Context, and Research Trajectory

---

### 1. THE PAPER

**Title:** A Mathematical Theory of Agency and Intelligence
**arXiv ID:** 2602.22519
**Submitted:** February 26, 2026; revised March 8, 2026
**Authors:** Wael Hafez, Chenan Wei, Rodrigo Pena, Amir Nazeri, Cameron Reid
**Length:** 20 pages, 4 figures
**Classification:** cs.AI, cs.IT

The paper introduces **bipredictability (P)** -- a measure of how much of the total information a system deploys is actually shared between its observations, actions, and outcomes. The authors prove that P is intrinsic to any interaction, derivable from first principles, and strictly bounded: it can reach 1.0 in quantum systems, is at most 0.5 in classical systems, and drops lower once agency (action selection) is introduced. They validate these bounds on three testbeds: a double pendulum (physical system), reinforcement learning agents, and multi-turn LLM conversations.

A key distinction drawn: **agency** is the capacity to act on predictions, while **intelligence** additionally requires learning from interaction, self-monitoring of learning effectiveness, and adaptive scope adjustment. By this definition, current AI systems achieve agency but not intelligence. The paper proposes a feedback architecture inspired by biological thalamocortical regulation for real-time monitoring of P.

### 2. SEMARX RESEARCH LLC

- **Website:** https://www.semarx.com/ (tagline: "AI safety and alignment")
- **Location:** Alexandria, Virginia
- **Size:** 5-9 employees; revenue estimated $1M-$5M (per ZoomInfo)
- **Founded/led by:** Wael Hafez (AI Digital Transformation Lead)

**Core research program: "Entanglement Learning" (EL)** -- an information-theoretic framework that introduces a structural information constraint enabling systems to maintain coherence across tasks and conditions. The operational core is the **Information Digital Twin (IDT)**, a non-invasive, modular monitoring layer that tracks how well a system's internal model stays aligned with its environment through the structure of information itself. The IDT operates as a parallel feedback layer that complements an agent's primary architecture without altering its task-specific components -- it can integrate into neural networks, RL agents, and other architectures.

The IDT also introduces a third behavioral mode called **SEEK**, extending beyond the classical exploration-exploitation duality. Unlike reactive strategies driven by external rewards or uncertainty, SEEK is initiated by the IDT to actively maximize information throughput between the agent and its environment.

The companion concept is the **Human Digital Twin (HDT)** -- a human-specific smart machine dedicated to aligning human objectives with the machines supporting them, creating two-way alignment. This was Hafez's original entry point into the research.

**Application domains** listed by Semarx: robotics, healthcare, finance, energy, autonomous vehicles, and infrastructure -- with emphasis on early drift detection and adaptive self-correction without retraining.

### 9. IBM PAPER: "AGENTIC AI NEEDS A SYSTEMS THEORY" (arXiv:2503.00237)

**Authors:** Erik Miehling, Karthikeyan Natesan Ramamurthy, Kush R. Varshney, Matthew Riemer, Djallel Bouneffouf, John T. Richards, Amit Dhurandhar, Elizabeth M. Daly, Michael Hind, Prasanna Sattigeri, Dennis Wei, Ambrish Rawat, Jasmina Gajcin, Werner Geyer
**Submitted:** February 28, 2025

The IBM paper defines **"functional agency"** along three dimensions: action generation (reactive to epistemic), outcome modeling (association to counterfactual), and adaptation (contextual to reflective). It argues for a systems-theoretic perspective on agentic AI, emphasizing emergent behaviors from interactions rather than individual capabilities. Three emergence mechanisms are proposed: enhanced cognition via environment, causal reasoning from prediction, and metacognition via interaction.

**Relationship to Hafez et al.:** The two papers address the same fundamental question -- what is agency and how should we understand it? -- but from different angles. The IBM paper is qualitative and draws from developmental psychology, neuroscience, and social dynamics. The Hafez et al. paper is mathematical, grounding agency in information theory with provable bounds. Neither paper cites the other directly. The IBM team also organized the FAST workshop where these themes were explored. The Hafez et al. paper represents an independent, parallel effort to formalize agency theory, but with a quantitative, information-theoretic foundation rather than a systems-theoretic one.

### 10. COMPANION PAPER: "BEYOND REWARD" (arXiv:2603.01283)

Published just 3 days after the main paper (March 1, 2026), this companion paper by Hafez, Reid, and Nazeri provides the empirical RL validation. It demonstrates the IDT computing bipredictability from interaction data on SAC and PPO agents in MuJoCo simulations, detecting 89.3% of perturbations versus 44.0% for reward-based monitoring, with substantially faster response times.

### 11. PRIOR PUBLICATIONS REFERENCED

- **Reid et al., 2025** (arXiv:2509.10423): "Mutual Information Tracks Policy Coherence in Reinforcement Learning" -- showed that state-action mutual information grows 238% during successful RL training and can diagnose sensor vs. actuator faults. This is a precursor establishing that information-theoretic measures track agent coherence.

- **Nazeri et al., 2025** (arXiv:2508.21715): "Entropy-Based Non-Invasive Reliability Monitoring of CNNs" -- demonstrated that adversarial perturbations create detectable entropy signatures in CNN activations (7% entropy shift in early layers, 90% detection accuracy), providing a non-invasive monitoring approach. This validates the entropy-monitoring component of the IDT.

- **Hafez, 2022** (AGI 2022, Springer LNCS 13539): "Information as Entanglement -- A Framework for AGI" -- the theoretical foundation. Argues that intelligence is an agent's continuous activity to maximize its entanglement (mutual information) with its environment, and introduces the Information Digital Twin as a mechanism for this.
