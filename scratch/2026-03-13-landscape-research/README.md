# Landscape Research — 2026-03-13

Research outputs from a deep comparative analysis session examining ACT against the broader landscape of unified agency frameworks.

## Context

This research emerged from a "hypothetical theory choice" exercise: if forced to choose between ACT and Hafez et al.'s "A Mathematical Theory of Agency and Intelligence" (2026), which contribution is more valuable? The exercise was designed to stress-test ACT's positioning, identify genuine competitors, find gaps, and extract a comprehensive bibliography.

The full discussion transcript (with metacognitive analysis of reasoning failures, bias correction, and methodological lessons) is in `../2026-03-13--hypothetical-theory-choice.md`.

## Files

### Core Research
- **`landscape-broad-theories.md`** — 25 frameworks identified and assessed across 3 tiers. Key finding: categorical cybernetics (Smithe/Hedges) and Active Inference (Friston) are the closest competitors in integrative ambition. No single framework covers ACT's full domain span.
- **`gap-analysis.md`** — 10 areas where existing literature is ahead of ACT (safety, game theory, causal RL, meta-learning, scalable multi-agent, etc.) with accommodation difficulty assessed for each.
- **`hafez-semarx-research.md`** — Deep background on the Hafez et al. authors, Semarx Research LLC, the 14-year research trajectory, and the companion paper "Beyond Reward."
- **`bibliography-extracted.md`** — Consolidated bibliography of ~150 references extracted from all research agents, organized by relevance tier and topic, with potential collaboration targets identified.

### Raw / Caveated
- **`novelty-analysis-raw.md`** — Prior art check on 10 ACT claims. **IMPORTANT CAVEAT**: This analysis was methodologically flawed. The research agent received paraphrased ACT claims stripped of context and epistemic labels, leading it to "debunk" claims ACT never made (e.g., treating the uncertainty ratio as a claimed novel formula when ACT explicitly identifies it as the Kalman gain). The analysis answers "is this formula new?" when ACT's actual novelty claim is about cross-domain integration. Retain for the references it surfaced, but do not trust the novelty verdicts. See the main discussion transcript for the full critique.
- **`act-author-research.md`** — Background research on ACT's author. Included for completeness.

## Key Findings

1. **ACT's cross-domain integration is genuinely unmatched** in the specific combination it targets (control theory + RL + multi-agent + adversarial dynamics + software engineering + agent taxonomy). The 25-framework survey confirmed no other framework covers this territory.

2. **Categorical cybernetics and structured active inference are real competitors** in mathematical sophistication and integrative ambition, but cover different domains (no software, no adversarial formalization, no accessible practitioner vocabulary).

3. **ACT has significant gaps** in safety/corrigibility, game theory, causal RL, and scalable multi-agent theory that the literature is actively addressing.

4. **The software domain work (Section IV) has no competitor** across all 25 frameworks surveyed.

5. **Hafez's P metric is genuinely novel** (more so than most of ACT's individual components) and complementary to ACT's framework.

6. **Most of ACT's individual mathematical components are established results** — which is a *strength* (solid foundations for synthesis), not a weakness, despite how the raw novelty analysis frames it.
