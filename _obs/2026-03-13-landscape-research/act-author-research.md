## The Intellectual Provenance

The founding notes document the intellectual lineage explicitly:

1. **TST** (January 2026): Formalizes 12 theorems about software development optimization around time as the fundamental metric. Written in a teaching voice; appears to codify ideas developed through years of engineering leadership.

2. **TFT** (February 2026): Generalizes from software to all adaptive systems. Formalizes the universal feedback loop (mismatch signal, update gain, adaptive tempo, persistence condition). Draws on control theory (Lyapunov, Kalman), Boyd's OODA loop, information theory (information bottleneck), and causal inference (Pearl).

3. **ACT** (March 2026): Synthesizes TFT and TST while adding goals/intent as first-class objects. Explicitly positions against BDI architecture (Rao & Georgeff), Active Inference (Friston), Hafez et al.'s bi-predictability (2026), and IBM's "Agentic AI Needs a Systems Theory" (2025). The founding notes are remarkably candid about what's borrowed vs. novel: "Don't present as revolutionary new mathematics. Present as a unifying framework with specific novel results embedded within the integration."

The work references and engages seriously with: Pearl/Bareinboim causal hierarchy, Lyapunov stability theory, Kalman filtering, Boyd's OODA loop, Auftragstaktik (mission-type tactics), Bungay's *Art of Action*, BDI (Belief-Desire-Intention) agent architecture, Friston's Free Energy Principle / Active Inference, and recent 2025-2026 papers from the AAAI FAST workshop.
