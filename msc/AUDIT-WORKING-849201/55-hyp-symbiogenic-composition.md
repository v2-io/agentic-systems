**1. Predictions vs evidence:**
My prediction was that this segment would describe the dynamical process by which a system moves from `#scope-multi-agent` into `#scope-composite-agent`, focusing on asymmetric integration (a host absorbing an endosymbiont). The segment delivered exactly this, formally breaking symbiogenesis into three coupled dynamics: (S-1) Objective absorption, (S-2) Function transfer, and (S-3) Autonomy reduction.

**2. Cross-segment consistency:**
The segment perfectly situates symbiogenesis within the broader theory. It explicitly distinguishes it from peer coupling (`#form-composition-closure`) and population-level extreme transitions (Miller 2022). Symbiogenesis is the *mechanism* of crossing the scope boundary; the other concepts apply *after* the boundary is crossed, or at a completely different scale.

**3. Math verification:**
The segment honestly labels itself a "Hypothesis" and "Robust qualitative," acknowledging that the specific dynamical equations for (S-1) and (S-2) are open. However, the sketch connecting (S-3) Autonomy reduction to the weighted-Lyapunov limit ($\mu \to 0$) in `#deriv-critical-mass-composition` is a brilliant piece of mathematical intuition. It shows that symbiogenesis isn't a magical discontinuous leap, but a smooth parameter limit of the standard peer-coupling stability proof, where one agent's autonomous correction weight goes to zero.

Modeling them as purely passive $M_t$ components would erase this residual agency.
