## Findings under burden of proof

### F1. `#def-adaptive-tempo` defines an unrestricted additive scalar that the same segment later demotes to an upper bound

**1. Problematic passage**

- `01-aad-core/src/def-adaptive-tempo.md:19` defines:
  `\mathcal T = \sum_k \nu^{(k)} \cdot \eta^{(k)\ast}`.
- `01-aad-core/src/def-adaptive-tempo.md:44-48` then says this assumes informationally independent channels and that under correlation the correct quantity satisfies only
  `\mathcal T \leq \sum_k \nu^{(k)} \cdot \eta^{(k)\ast}`.

**2. Counterevidence search**

- The caveat is not hidden. The segment states it explicitly.
- `01-aad-core/src/der-team-persistence.md:32` propagates the same caveat to communication tempo.
- `01-aad-core/src/result-persistence-condition.md:99` also notes that scalar tempo inherits the channel-independence assumption.
- `01-aad-core/src/disc-independence-audit.md:59` records the same repair in even more direct language.
- `msc/pending-findings-2026-04-23.md:164` appears to have already logged this concern in `msc/`.

**3. Status determination**

- **Still real.**

**4. Confidence**

- **High.**

**5. Why it still stands**

- The repair exists as prose, but the segment still presents the unrestricted equality as the primary definition and carries `status: exact`.
- That means the main load-bearing quantity is still named in a form that is only exact in a restricted sub-scope.

**Diagnosis**

- This looks like **integration debt / definition-scope mismatch**, not a deep missing theorem.
- The clean repair path is to distinguish:
  - a general effective tempo quantity
  - from the additive surrogate / upper bound valid under channel independence

### F2. The Model-S persistence summary layer still compresses away Prop A.1S's region-awareness too aggressively

- `msc/SPIKES.md:107` and `msc/spike-a2-prime-strengthening.md:142-143` show the project has already thought about how far that repair needed to propagate.

**3. Status determination**

**4. Confidence**

- **High / medium-high.**

**5. Why it still stands**

**Diagnosis**

- This is **integration debt / scope-honesty drift**, not a flaw in Prop A.1S itself.
- My audit judgment differs from the prior `msc/` judgment only on propagation depth: I think the current repair stops one layer too early.

### F3. The new C-iv strategic-composite route is only partially integrated, and some downstream composition machinery still overstates what it covers

**1. Problematic passage**

- `01-aad-core/src/scope-composite-agent.md:46-63` admits route (C-iv): strategic composites whose macro-state is defined relative to equilibrium structure `\mathcal E`, not shared objective `O_c`.
- `01-aad-core/src/scope-multi-agent.md:73` says the (C-iv) strategic route admits composite-level machinery.
- But `01-aad-core/src/form-composition-closure.md:65` still says the closure-defect framework applies via "the three alignment routes" and treats lack of well-defined `G_c = (O_c, \Sigma_c)` as the reason composition-level machinery does not apply.
- `01-aad-core/src/def-unity-dimensions.md:19` and `:40` still describe the scope condition as a three-route disjunction.
- Most importantly, `01-aad-core/src/deriv-strategic-composition.md:119`, `:135`, and `:175` explicitly say the bridge from strategic composition to `#form-composition-closure`'s closure-defect machinery is still open.

There is even some residual tension inside `#scope-composite-agent` itself:

- formal route (C-iv) says strategic composites need not have shared `O_c`
- but the scope rationale at `01-aad-core/src/scope-composite-agent.md:79-81` still justifies composite status in terms of whether shared-objective `G_c = (O_c, \Sigma_c)` is well-defined

**2. Counterevidence search**

- This is not a case where the framework simply forgot C-iv.
- `#scope-composite-agent` does have the route.
- `#scope-multi-agent` does distinguish agent-level from strategic-composite machinery.
- `#deriv-strategic-composition` is candid that the closure-defect bridge remains open.
- `msc/spike-strategic-composition.md:443` already stated the same open bridge.
- `msc/spike-bridge-lemma-nonlinear-strengthening-2026-04-24.md:297` sharpens the adversarial/strategic regime as structurally outside the contraction-template bridge.
- `msc/pending-findings-2026-04-25.md:109` points to a larger architectural split option (`SP-21`).

**3. Status determination**

- **Still real.**

**4. Confidence**

- **High.**

**5. Why it still stands**

- The corpus is currently saying all of the following at once:
  - C-iv composites are in scope
  - C-iv composites have equilibrium-based macro-state rather than shared `O_c`
  - some closure / unity machinery still presumes shared-objective composites
  - and the strategic-composition segment itself says the closure-defect bridge is still open

**Diagnosis**

- This is the most interesting finding of the pass because it is **not** just stale wording and **not** just an unproven theorem.
- It is a **hybrid**:
  - **integration debt** where route-count / scope language / downstream references still reflect the older alignment-only frame
  - **theory-open component** where the closure-defect ontology genuinely does not yet fit equilibrium-statistic composites cleanly

## Integration-debt diagnosis via `msc/`

### F1

`msc/` strongly suggests this is already understood as a repairable propagation problem, not a new research gap.

- `01-aad-core/src/disc-independence-audit.md:59` states the exact repair operation
- `msc/pending-findings-2026-04-23.md:164` appears to track it historically

My recommendation is local and modest:

- either scope the equality definition explicitly to channel independence
- or rename the additive scalar as an upper-bound surrogate and reserve `\mathcal T` for the general effective quantity

### F2

`msc/` suggests a different story:

- the project seems to have consciously chosen to lift region-awareness into Prop A.1S and add a template-level note, while leaving downstream consumer summaries largely unchanged

I think that choice undershoots what the current corpus needs. This is still an integration issue, but the issue is not lack of awareness; it is the current decision about how much summary compression is acceptable.

### F3

`msc/` shows both a local and a global path already on the table:

- **local path:** tighten the existing segments so they stop implying closure-defect / shared-`O_c` machinery applies unchanged to C-iv composites
- **global path:** the architectural restructure already sketched in `SP-21`, where different composite routes get distinct macro-objects and theorem families

I do not think the audit should choose between those paths. But I do think the current `src` state is exactly the kind of half-landed architecture change that deserves to be called out.

## Bigger-picture assessment

### Where the framework felt strongest

The strongest thing in the corpus I read is still the **discipline of local caveating in the best segments**:

- Section I's persistence machinery is materially stronger than the surrounding rhetoric needs it to be
- the satisfaction-gap / control-regret split remains one of the clearest and most useful structural moves in Section II
- the current logogenic-agent cluster is notably more self-aware than I expected
- the TST developer-agent mapping appears to have absorbed the major Class-2 caveats rather than pretending modularity where it does not hold

This matters because it changes the shape of the audit: most of what I found was **not** "segment says something reckless that the next paragraph denies." The problems clustered more in:

- summary compression
- cross-segment propagation around recent additions
- ontology strain where one theorem family is being asked to cover more than it cleanly can

### Where the framework still feels over-ambitious relative to its current segment set

Section III is the clearest example. It now wants to unify:

- aligned composites
- mutual-benefit composites
- strategic / equilibrium composites
- closure-defect coarse-graining
- contraction-style bridge lemmas
- signed coupling and adversarial regimes

That is an exciting direction, but the current segment set still shows the strain of asking one composite ontology to do multiple jobs.

My own strongest bigger-picture hypothesis after this pass is:

- the framework may become cleaner if it stops trying to make every composite route share the same macro-object and theorem family

I am not promoting that to a formal finding, but F3 pushes in that direction.

## Process feedback on the new audit instructions

The new instructions are good. They materially improved the quality of this pass. The strongest moves were:

- forcing predictions before segment reading
- forcing `src`-first source ordering
- making cross-segment drift a first-class audit target instead of an afterthought

### Concrete polish suggestions

**1. Add an explicit SCC / cycle-handling clause.**

The instructions currently recommend topological reading order as if the active frontmatter graph will be acyclic. In current `src`, it is not: the strategic-tempo cluster still contains a 7-segment SCC. The instructions should explicitly say what to do when the graph is cyclic:

- identify the SCC
- read conceptual / weaker-status nodes first
- then read supporting derivations
- then revisit the earlier nodes and judge whether the cycle is honest support or frontmatter-design debt

I ended up doing exactly this, but only because the repo forced it.

**2. Clarify the `CLAUDE.md` bleed problem.**

The instructions say to read `CLAUDE.md` early. That is understandable, but in the current repo `CLAUDE.md` is not a thin conventions file; it contains substantial spoiler-level architectural state. I think the instructions should say this more explicitly and perhaps suggest:

- write the first half of `00-initial-predictions.md` before reading `CLAUDE.md`, or
- read `CLAUDE.md` but explicitly log the bleed immediately

I did the latter. It worked, but it would be better if the instructions anticipated it.

**3. Mark Appendix A's example findings as historical calibration, not presumed-live examples.**

At least some appendix examples are no longer live in current `src`:

- the TST/logogenic developer-agent issue
- the strategic-composition sign error
- the discrete-time variance-gap wording

The appendix is still useful as a pattern library, but it should probably say more clearly:

- these were once-real findings
- they may already be repaired
- they are calibration examples, not current repo claims

Otherwise the appendix can quietly bias the "de novo" posture it is trying to protect.

**4. Say explicitly that component-local outline linting may misread cross-component dependencies.**

In this repo, `bin/lint-outline` is still useful, but not sufficient on its own because cross-component references look like missing deps to the component-local pass. The instructions could save future agents some confusion by saying "prefer corpus-wide dependency analysis when available."

## Bottom line

This pass did **not** surface a fundamental collapse of the framework's core mathematical story.

It **did** surface three real issues:

1. a definition-scope mismatch around scalar adaptive tempo
2. a summary-layer over-compression of the stochastic persistence theorem's region-awareness
3. a still-live partial integration of the C-iv strategic-composite route, with one genuinely open ontology/theorem-family question underneath it

The pattern is consistent:

- Section I / II core is relatively disciplined
- current weaknesses are mostly at the seams:
  recent scope additions,
  summary compression,
  and places where the framework wants one formal object to carry more than it currently can
