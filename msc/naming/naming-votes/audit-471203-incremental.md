## Extraction notes

**Categories of friction the audit surfaced most often:**

1. **Slug-as-mechanical-prefix-hides-substantive-claim** (the dominant pattern). The auditor repeatedly hit segments where the slug followed the role-prefix-+-subject-noun discipline mechanically but the *substantive* contribution lived in a phrase the slug omitted. `scope-agent-identity` (hides "Identity as Singular Causal Trajectory") is the strongest case; `der-recursive-update` (hides "Markov-by-Completeness"), `der-gain-sector-bridge` (hides "The Bridge Theorem" cross-tradition unification), `post-composition-consistency` (hides "scale invariance forcing tempo composition rules") follow. This pattern wasn't caught by the deliberate r2 voters as systematically — the auditor felt it *as cognitive cost across multiple segments*, which the trajectory-empathy gradient surfaced naturally.

2. **Term-vs-substance mismatch.** Most acute on **CIY** (the segment goes to substantial trouble to clarify it measures action-distinguishability not learning value, but the term still misleads); also on `def-agent-environment` (segment is about the *boundary*, not the *coupling*); also milder on "model class fitness," "model sufficiency," "update gain."

3. **Forgettable-but-structurally-important.** "Nominal coupling" was the strongest; the auditor traced it forward into TST-queries-as-interventions and into logogenic-agent attention-as-action and judged that the term was carrying more weight than the words suggested. "Transition opacity" was a softer instance.

4. **Greek-vocabulary-load-bearing-ness verdict.** The auditor reached an explicit verdict (segment 18) that the Greek vocabulary in mismatch-signal/aporia is *pedagogical only* — the formalism doesn't use the Greek terms structurally. He flagged this as making the README claim ("each names a distinction the formalism makes that English alternatives flatten") *overclaimed*. By extension, this judgment applies to the cycle phases (predict/observe/mismatch/update/act). This is a finding the deliberate r2 voters do not seem to have produced as a verdict — it requires reading enough of the formalism to notice the Greek terms aren't structurally load-bearing, which the trajectory-empathy walk produces naturally.

5. **Unnamed concepts the segments use but don't name.** Most prominent: the **constitutive-opacity triad / double-opacity** is a structural commitment running across segments 1–3 that the framework never names as a triad. The auditor's empathy-gradient observation was that a fresh reader might be told "$\Omega$ is unrestricted" and miss the constitutive-scope move. Also: **predictive completeness vs behavioral completeness** as two distinct properties bundled in C3. Also: **persistence taxonomy** as a four-way split (structural / task / operational / continuity) that lives in LEXICON but isn't surfaced in framing-level material.

**Distinctive contributions vs the deliberate r2 voters (read after extraction):**
