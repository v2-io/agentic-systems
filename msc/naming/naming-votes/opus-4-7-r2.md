# Naming Votes — Opus 4.7 (refined Round 1)

**Agent:** `opus-4-7-r2` (Anthropic Claude Opus 4.7, 1M context)

**Approach:** I focused on three things, in roughly this order. (1) Subject-nouns that name *roles* rather than *things* — the slug-form principle from the pilot — which I think still has a long tail in the corpus (`-pattern`, `-condition`, `-modulation`, `-classification`, `-mapping`, `-decomposition`, `-principle` placeholders that survive in slugs because they were defensible at promotion-time but read as scaffolding now). (2) Symbol-to-English aliases for Greek-letter and subscript-laden quantities that show up in prose without an English form to lean on (α, ρ, R, Δρ\*, ε\*, U_M / U_O / U_Σ / U_f, ι, A, κ_processing). I think this layer is currently very thin and is where most genuinely-load-bearing reading-friction lives. (3) Name-unnamed-thing votes for recurring patterns and regions that the framework keeps reaching for in prose without a name — the persistence envelope, the calibration laboratory disclosure, the four-axis architecture-content split, the convention-conditioned diagnostic pair, and similar.

I tried to be vocal about *keeps* I thought were genuinely load-bearing — names whose churn I would actively oppose — and to use the +2 weight where the strength of conviction is visibly between weak and strong, per the principles file's note on the +1/+3 collapse. I have explicit `-1` votes on candidate replacements I considered and rejected; these matter for the aggregation as much as the positive votes do.

I avoided the `naming/` subdirectory entirely (cold-start), and I did not look at `git status`, `PRACTICA.md`, `TODO.md`, or `PROPOSALS.md` either, since those carry priming. I have one disclosure: I read the project-level `CLAUDE.md` (auto-loaded as project instructions in this session) and `MEMORY.md` (auto-loaded user memory). Both appear in the system harness, not as a deliberate read. They mention the slug-form principle and the recent pilot-rename outcomes (e.g., `#scope-condition` → `#scope-adaptive-system` + `#scope-agency`) but do not name specific Round-1 vote contents or the contents of the brainstorm / aggregate files. I think the priming is light but it is honest to disclose.

## Principles-observations

**On the +2 weight scale.** I used +2 considerably for "acceptable keep" (a defensible keep against a reasonable rename) and "moderate-strength rename" (visible improvement, not load-bearing). This produced a fair number of +2 votes — possibly more than the principles file anticipated. The scale's value is real; the +1/+3 collapse the principles file mentions was a genuine problem.

**On absence of a vote.** I tried to be vocal about explicit keeps to give the aggregation signal, but I'm aware I haven't covered every segment in the corpus (~110 in 01-aad-core/ alone, plus TST and logogenic). I focused my keep-votes on slugs I considered renaming and chose to keep (i.e., where my keep is *informed*), and on slugs that the principles file's example sections flagged as load-bearing. Slugs I didn't touch represent "no opinion expressed," not endorsement.

**On role-prefix discipline.** I followed the architectural-invariant rule strictly: every vote operates on the subject-noun layer or below. I did not propose role-prefix changes. That said, two segments have type-vs-role-prefix mismatches I noticed: `disc-additive-coordinate-forcing` is typed `discussion` and prefixed `disc-` correctly; `def-the-four-views` and `def-the-creche-boundary` use the `def-` prefix consistently. The "drop the article" rename votes I cast are subject-noun-only; they don't touch the type prefix.

**On the symbol-overload disclosure.** $U_M$ is genuinely overloaded (model uncertainty in single-agent contexts; epistemic unity in multi-agent contexts). I voted both as add-aliases and surfaced it as an unnamed-thing entry. Whether the right resolution is rename-one-symbol or canonicalize-via-context-discipline is beyond the scope of this round (it would touch the symbol layer, which feels architectural rather than naming).

**On the "drop 'the' from slugs" votes.** A handful of logozoetic segments (`def-the-four-views`, `def-the-creche-boundary`, `der-the-scaffolding-tax`, `hyp-the-three-deaths`) include articles in their slugs, departing from the rest of the corpus. I voted to drop the articles for stylistic consistency. This may be too mechanical for the principles file's "judgment-heavy" framing, but it's a real consistency issue.

**On the cold-start instruction.** Worked for me as written. I did read `CLAUDE.md` and the auto-loaded `MEMORY.md` (disclosed above), but neither names specific Round-1 vote contents — they describe the project's architectural commitments and the pilot's outcomes, which are architectural-invariant inputs the principles file says to honor. I avoided every file under `msc/naming/` and did not look at `git status` (which the principles file specifically warned about).

**On things the principles file is under-specified about.** Two minor points: (1) the principles file's example for "rename" includes a `-1` rejection vote on the same target; the format works fine but it would be clarifying to mention that *multiple votes per current-name* (different new-name candidates ranked by weight) are an expected pattern, not a quirk. **On things I'd flag for the principles writer.** The Greek-vocabulary commitment is named in CLAUDE.md but the principles file mentions it briefly in the architectural-invariants section. Strengthening the rule that *new* naming proposals should sit within (or deliberately depart from with rationale) the Greek-philosophical aesthetic register would be useful — I caught myself testing some of my candidate renames against this register, but a clearer prompt would have made my own scrutiny more consistent.
