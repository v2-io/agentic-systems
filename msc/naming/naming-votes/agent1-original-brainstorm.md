## Principles-observations

The original brainstorm noted three meta-observations worth flagging to the principles file:

1. **AAD's naming is stronger at the conceptual level than at the meta-structural level.** Individual concepts (satisfaction gap, chronica, orient cascade) are well-named; meta-structure (the three cross-cutting patterns, the A2' sub-scope partition) is less-well-named. This is the normal trajectory — conceptual work comes first, meta-structural work comes later, and naming lags.

2. **AAD has a latent Greek-vocabulary commitment that could be made explicit.** Chronica, prolepsis, aisthesis, aporia, epistrophe, praxis, logogenic, logozoetic — the project uses Greek roots for its most-load-bearing conceptual names. Stating the commitment explicitly would help future renames honor it.

3. **The "communal imagination" test correlates with memorable-noun-slot ownership, not with self-descriptiveness.** An evocative imprecise name beats a precise forgettable name. Names that pass: satisfaction gap, control regret, chronica, orient cascade, identifiability floor, directed separation. Names that fail: additive-coordinate-forcing, separability-pattern, A2' sub-scope α₁/α₂/β, convention hierarchy.
