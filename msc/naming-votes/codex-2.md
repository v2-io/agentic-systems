## Principles Observations

- I repeatedly made decisions using a "say it out loud six months later" test. That is adjacent to memorable-noun potential and communal imagination, but worth naming more explicitly.
- Slug hygiene deserves explicit weight. A prose title can be fine while the slug still creates recurring friction; `act-agent` and `ASF` are the clearest examples.
- There is a useful distinction between names that can afford Greek/Latinate strangeness because they buy a unique noun (`chronica`, `logogenic`) and names that are merely clinical compounds. I relied on that distinction often.
