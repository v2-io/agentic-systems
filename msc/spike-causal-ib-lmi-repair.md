**Pressure Point.** The original derivation in `msc/spike-causal-information-bottleneck.md` (and promoted to `01-aad-core/src/deriv-causal-ib-exploration.md`) proved that survival forces an exploration drive ($\lambda_{\text{surv}} \propto 1/U_M$) to bound scalar observation noise $U_o$. 

**The Adversarial Break:** A scalar bound on trace variance allows pathological "exploration." An agent can artificially minimize scalar $U_o$ by repeatedly taking trivial actions with highly certain outcomes (e.g., staring at a blank wall), satisfying the mathematical constraint without ever probing the actual environmental drift $\rho$. To survive multi-dimensional drift, the agent must gather information in the *specific directions* where the environment is changing.

This spike repairs the derivation by upgrading the scalar Lyapunov constraint to a Linear Matrix Inequality (LMI) over the Fisher Information Matrix (FIM).

## 1. The Multi-Dimensional Mismatch Dynamics

Let the mismatch state be a vector $\delta \in \mathbb{R}^n$.
The discrete-time or locally-linearized update dynamics for the epistemic state $M_t$ under a Kalman-like gain $K$ are:
$$ \delta_{t+1} = (I - K H) \delta_t + w_t - K v_t $$
Where:
- $H$ is the observation matrix.
- $w_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, Q_\rho)$ is the environmental drift/disturbance.
- $v_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, R_o)$ is the observation noise for the chosen action $a_t$.
- $K = P H^T (H P H^T + R_o)^{-1}$ is the optimal update gain (with $P$ as the agent's prior uncertainty covariance $U_M$).

The steady-state mismatch covariance $\Sigma_\delta$ satisfies the discrete-time Lyapunov equation. For the agent to survive, the maximum eigenvalue of $\Sigma_\delta$ must remain strictly bounded by its structural capacity: $\lambda_{\max}(\Sigma_\delta) \lt R^2$.

## 2. The Fisher Information Matrix (FIM) and CIY

The amount of information an action $a_t$ provides about the state is quantified by the Fisher Information Matrix $\mathcal{I}_o(a_t)$. For Gaussian linear observations, $\mathcal{I}_o(a_t) = H^T R_o^{-1} H$.

The update to the agent's precision (inverse covariance, $P^{-1}$) is exactly additive in Fisher Information:
$$ P_{t+1}^{-1} = P_t^{-1} + \mathcal{I}_o(a_t) $$

In `#def-causal-information-yield`, CIY was defined as a scalar surrogate for EIG. In the multi-dimensional case, **Matrix CIY** is exactly the interventional Fisher Information Matrix generated by the action.

## 3. The LMI Survival Constraint

To keep the steady-state mismatch bounded against a specific directional disturbance covariance $Q_\rho$, the agent's precision must counteract the drift. 

Using standard Riccati inequality bounds for filter stability, bounded error requires that the observability Gramian (which integrates the Fisher Information over time) dominates the unstabilizable modes of the system drift.

Specifically, to ensure $\lambda_{\max}(\Sigma_\delta) \lt R^2$, the Fisher Information Matrix generated by the policy $\pi$ must satisfy the **Linear Matrix Inequality (LMI)** constraint:
$$ \mathbb{E}_{a \sim \pi}[\mathcal{I}_o(a)] \succeq \mathcal{I}_{\min}(P_t, Q_\rho, R^2) $$
where $\mathcal{I}_{\min}$ is a symmetric positive-definite matrix representing the minimum directional information required to stabilize the specific drift profile $Q_\rho$.

*(Translation: You cannot just collect total scalar information; your Fisher Information Matrix must "cover" the eigenspace of the environmental drift.)*

## 4. The Tensor Lagrangian and the True Exploration Objective

The agent's objective is to maximize pragmatic value $Q_O(a)$ subject to the LMI constraint:
$$ \max_{\pi} \mathbb{E}_{a \sim \pi} [Q_O(a)] $$
$$ \text{s.t. } \mathbb{E}_{a \sim \pi}[\mathcal{I}_o(a)] - \mathcal{I}_{\min} \succeq 0 $$

For LMI constraints, the Lagrange multiplier is not a scalar $\lambda$, but a **positive semi-definite matrix $\Lambda \succeq 0$**. 

The KKT Lagrangian becomes:
$$ \mathcal{L} = \mathbb{E}[Q_O(a)] + \text{Tr}\left( \Lambda \cdot \mathbb{E}[\mathcal{I}_o(a)] \right) $$

### The Structure of $\Lambda$ (The Directional Exploration Drive)
The matrix Lagrange multiplier $\Lambda$ is the "shadow price" of the LMI constraint. By complementary slackness:
$$ \text{Tr}\left( \Lambda \cdot (\mathbb{E}[\mathcal{I}_o(a)] - \mathcal{I}_{\min}) \right) = 0 $$

When the agent's uncertainty in a specific eigen-direction $v$ approaches the fatal bound $R^2$, the constraint becomes active along that direction. The matrix $\Lambda$ will develop a massive principal eigenvalue corresponding to eigenvector $v$.

The agent's action selection rule becomes:
$$ a_t = \arg\max_a \left[ Q_O(a) + \text{Tr}\left( \Lambda \cdot \mathcal{I}_o(a) \right) \right] $$

## 5. Conclusion and Resolution of the "Blank Wall" Paradox

By replacing the scalar $\lambda \cdot \text{CIY}$ with the trace inner-product $\text{Tr}(\Lambda \cdot \mathcal{I}_o(a))$, the math perfectly resolves the adversarial critique:
1. **The Blank Wall Fails:** If an action provides high information (low noise) but only about an irrelevant, non-drifting dimension (e.g., staring at a blank wall), the Fisher Information matrix $\mathcal{I}_o(a)$ will have a large eigenvalue in the "wall" direction. However, the shadow price matrix $\Lambda$ will have *zero* weight in that direction, because the "wall" dimension is not threatening survival. The trace product $\text{Tr}(\Lambda \cdot \mathcal{I}_o)$ will evaluate to 0. The action is useless.
2. **Directional Exploration:** The agent is mathematically forced to choose actions whose Fisher Information matrix aligns with the principal eigenvectors of $\Lambda$. It must probe the exact causal dimensions that are currently drifting out of control.

**Impact:** This completes the derivation of Causal-IB. The "exploration bonus" is a full matrix projection that forces the agent to ask the specific causal questions required to survive the environment's specific drift profile.
