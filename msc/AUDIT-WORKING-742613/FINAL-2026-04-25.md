## Findings

### Finding 1 — Score-function mismatch has reversed sign

Status: still real.
Confidence: high.
Type: mathematical sign error.

Problematic passage:

- `01-aad-core/src/def-mismatch-signal.md:33` defines `$\tilde{\delta}_t = -\nabla_M \log P(o_t \mid M_{t-1}, a_{t-1})$`.
- `01-aad-core/src/def-mismatch-signal.md:35` says this "points in the direction the model should move to increase the likelihood" and that under Gaussian models it coincides with `$\delta_t$` up to scaling.

Counterevidence / check:

For a scalar Gaussian observation model $o \sim \mathcal N(M,\sigma^2)$,

$$
\log P(o\mid M) = -\frac{(o-M)^2}{2\sigma^2}+c,
$$

so

$$
\nabla_M \log P(o\mid M)=\frac{o-M}{\sigma^2}=\frac{\delta}{\sigma^2}.
$$

The likelihood-increasing direction is $+\nabla_M\log P$, not $-\nabla_M\log P$.
The segment's definition gives $-\delta/\sigma^2$, the opposite of the stated Gaussian-equivalent direction.

Counterevidence search in read corpus:

- `result-mismatch-decomposition` uses the vector prediction error `$\delta_t=o_t-\hat o_t$`, not the score mismatch, so it does not repair or propagate the sign.
- `emp-update-gain` uses `$g(\delta_t)$` abstractly, so it also does not repair the sign.
- `deriv-sector-condition` and `deriv-gain-sector` use abstract correction functions / gradients, not this score definition directly.

Why it still stands:

The local definition and interpretation are inconsistent under the segment's own Gaussian claim.
The repair is simple: define score mismatch as `$\nabla_M\log P(...)$` if it is an ascent/update direction, or keep `-$\nabla\log P$` but call it negative-log-likelihood gradient and ensure downstream update rules subtract it.

### Finding 2 — Model S local stochastic persistence confuses fixed-time tail bounds with ever-exit probability

Status: still real.
Confidence: high.
Type: stochastic-process / Lyapunov scope error.

Problematic passages:

- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-sector-condition.md:180` introduces first-exit time `$\tau_R$` from the local sector ball.
- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-sector-condition.md:194` claims a Markov tail bound gives `$P(\tau_R \lt \infty) \leq n\sigma_w^2/(2\alpha R^2)$`, and that the unstopped bound holds up to a higher-order correction.
- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-sector-condition.md:242` says standard tail estimates yield the non-exit probability.
- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-sector-condition.md:253` and `:270` summarize the non-exit probability as proved.
- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-sector-condition.md:282` says stopping-time localization handles Wiener excursions beyond `$\mathcal B_R$` cleanly and no implicit strengthening of A2' is required.
- `01-aad-core/src/result-sector-persistence-template.md:47` states Model S mean-square convergence under local T2.
- `01-aad-core/src/result-sector-persistence-template.md:90` says instantiations inherit stopped bound plus non-exit probability.
- `01-aad-core/src/result-persistence-condition.md` imports the Model S structural threshold as exact in the central persistence result.

Counterevidence / check:

The stopped bound is plausible:

$$
\mathbb E[\lVert \delta(t\wedge\tau_R)\rVert^2]\leq \lVert\delta(0)\rVert^2 e^{-2\alpha t}+\frac{n\sigma_w^2}{2\alpha}.
$$

The invalid step is converting a fixed-time or stationary tail bound into an infinite-horizon non-exit probability.
For the segment's own linear scalar special case,

$$
d\delta=-\alpha\delta\,dt+\sigma_w\,dW_t,
$$

the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process has stationary Gaussian distribution with unbounded support.
For any finite $R$ and nonzero $\sigma_w$, the process exits `[-R,R]` eventually with probability 1.
Thus `$P(\tau_R<\infty)=1$`, while the claimed bound can be made much smaller than 1 by taking `$\alpha R^2 \gg \sigma_w^2$`.

What Markov's inequality can bound is `$P(\lVert\delta(t)\rVert>R)$` at a fixed time, or under a stationary distribution at a randomly sampled time.
It does not bound the probability of ever exiting over an infinite horizon.

Counterevidence search in read corpus:

- `hyp-mismatch-dynamics` correctly gives the OU RMS scaling for the scalar global linear case.
- `result-sector-condition-stability` states RMS and mean-square persistence more cautiously than the appendix, but depends on the template and appendix.
- `result-sector-persistence-template` repeats the non-exit inheritance.
- `result-persistence-condition` treats Model S structural thresholds as exact, inheriting the problem.

Why it still stands:

The exact deterministic sector result survives, and the stochastic RMS scaling survives under global linear/global-sector assumptions.
What fails is the local-region persistence guarantee for Brownian noise.
Repair options: state only the stopped bound; require global sector condition for unstopped mean-square convergence; use finite-horizon exit probability; use bounded stochastic disturbances; or frame `R_S^\ast<R` as a typical-scale condition rather than an infinite-horizon persistence guarantee.

### Finding 3 — Gradient "equivalence" overstates one-point sector as local strong convexity

Status: still real.
Confidence: high.
Type: mathematical equivalence error.

Problematic passages:

- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-gain-sector.md:127` introduces Proposition B.4 as "Gradient Equivalence."
- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-gain-sector.md:133` states `GA-3 holds with $(\alpha,R)$ iff $L$ is locally $(\alpha/\eta)$-strongly convex`.
- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-gain-sector.md:149` quotes the correct two-point gradient-monotonicity characterization of strong convexity.
- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-gain-sector.md:157` claims the one-point condition obtained by setting `$y=M^\ast$` is identical to the sector condition and that equivalence follows.
- `01-aad-core/src/deriv-gain-sector.md:261` says the bidirectional implication is proved.

Counterevidence / check:

Strong convexity implies the one-point sector condition, but the reverse does not follow.
The quoted theorem requires, for all `$x,y$` in the set:

$$
(\nabla L(x)-\nabla L(y))^T(x-y)\ge \mu\lVert x-y\rVert^2.
$$

The sector condition only gives the special case against the optimum:

$$
\delta^T\nabla L(M^\ast+\delta)\ge\mu\lVert\delta\rVert^2.
$$

That is one-point strong monotonicity / star-convex-like inwardness, not full local strong convexity.

A simple one-dimensional counterexample shape:

$$
L'(x)=x(1+\tfrac12\sin(10x)).
$$

Then `$xL'(x)\ge \tfrac12x^2$`, so the one-point sector condition holds.
But

$$
L''(x)=1+\tfrac12\sin(10x)+5x\cos(10x)
$$

is negative at some points in modest neighborhoods, so `$L$` is not locally strongly convex there.

Counterevidence search in read corpus:

- `result-sector-persistence-template.md:72` correctly describes AAD's sector condition as one-point anchoring and strictly weaker than full two-point strong monotonicity.
- That sentence actually supports the audit finding: the bridge appendix's iff claim is stronger than the framework's own meta-description.
- `deriv-sector-condition` only needs the one-point sector condition, so the persistence chain can be repaired without losing the core Lyapunov result.

Why it still stands:

The proof's reverse direction proves only the `$y=M^\ast$` instance of gradient monotonicity, not the all-pairs condition required for strong convexity.
Repair: make strong convexity a sufficient condition; define B1/one-point sector as the actual necessary condition for AAD persistence; or strengthen the required condition to incremental/two-point monotonicity where contraction/composition needs it.

Secondary implication:

`01-aad-core/src/deriv-gain-sector.md:173` treats exponential-family natural parameters as globally exact via `$\lambda_{\min}(\text{Fisher})$`.
Pointwise Fisher positive-definiteness does not imply a uniform global lower bound.
For Poisson natural parameter `$\theta$`, Fisher information is `$e^\theta$`, whose infimum over `$\mathbb R$` is 0.
Global sector constants need compact/interior bounded-away-from-boundary scope or an explicit uniform Fisher lower bound.

### Finding 8 — Model sufficiency is undefined when the full history has zero predictive information

Status: still real.
Confidence: medium.
Type: mathematical edge-case / missing well-definedness condition.

Problematic passages:

- `def-model-sufficiency.md:19` defines `$S(M_t)$` as a ratio whose denominator is `$I(\mathcal C_t; o_{t+1:\infty}\mid a_{t:\infty})$`.
- `def-model-sufficiency.md:23` identifies that denominator as total predictive information in the full history.
- `def-model-sufficiency.md:25-28` states boundary values for `$S=1,0$` without a denominator-positive condition.

Counterevidence / check:

If the full chronica has no predictive information about future observations under the conditioned action sequence, the denominator is zero.
That can occur in saturated-noise, iid, or otherwise prediction-vacuous regimes.
Then `$S(M_t)$` is undefined unless a convention is supplied.

Counterevidence search in read corpus:

- README orientation has a "degenerate interior" / saturated-noise corner, so the framework already knows such regimes can occur.
- `def-model-class-fitness` inherits the issue because it takes a supremum over `$S(M)$`.
- `result-structural-adaptation-necessity` inherits it via `$\mathcal F(\mathcal M)$`.

Why it still stands:

This is easy to repair but should be explicit.
Add the well-definedness condition `$I(\mathcal C_t; o_{t+1:\infty}\mid a_{t:\infty})>0$`, or define a convention for no-predictive-information regimes.
