# Consolidated External Review — 2026-03-13

Three independent reviews of AAD by frontier models (Claude Opus, OpenAI Codex, Google Gemini), consolidated here with attribution. Claude reviewed the actual segment files; Codex and Gemini reviewed assembled/rendered versions. The reviews converge strongly on the same core issues, which increases confidence that these are real.

**Purpose:** This document identifies what needs to change in the theory and its presentation. It is the primary input for the next round of work.


## What all three reviews agree is strong

- **Section I's adaptive core** is the most defensible part of the theory. The derivation chain from definitions through sector-condition stability is clean, rigorous, and well-structured.
- **The satisfaction-gap / control-regret split** is the most useful original formalization. The 2×2 diagnostic table is load-bearing and operational.
- **The epistemic honesty** is extraordinary and rare. Every segment labels what's exact, conditional, or discussion-grade. Working notes flag genuine open issues. This is better epistemic hygiene than most published work.
- **The orient cascade as information-forced ordering** is elegant — derived, not postulated. Two lines of reasoning, and the ordering follows.
- **The acyclicity derivation** from temporal ordering is tight and resolves a listed known fragility.
- **The synthesis framing** (known tools, novel integration) is the right way to present this. Do not overclaim novelty on individual pieces.


## Issues, ranked by severity

### 1. HIGH — Directed separation blocks the main application class

**Source:** All three reviewers independently flagged this as the most important theoretical gap.

The clean Section II architecture (sequential orient cascade, modular $M_t$-first update, independence of epistemic machinery from purposeful content) depends on directed separation: $f_M$ is $G_t$-independent. But LLM/logogenic agents violate this condition — their epistemic processing is goal-conditioned through the prompt. The theory explicitly acknowledges this and then proceeds to build Section II results that depend on it.

**The problem:** AAD currently has a strong theory for goal-blind epistemic updaters, not yet for goal-conditioned agents. Section V is blocked on the coupled alternative.

**What's needed:**
- Quantify the approximation error. Introduce a coupling strength $\kappa$ measuring how much $G_t$ leaks into $f_M$. Show what happens to the orient cascade as $\kappa$ increases from 0 (perfect separation) toward 1 (full coupling). Even a qualitative analysis would convert a binary scope condition into a graded one. (Claude)
- Treat logogenic agents as a distinct regime where $f_M(M_t, G_t, e_\tau)$ is fundamentally coupled. Prove that the sequential orient cascade is a special case for systems with independent sensory arrays, while logogenic agents operate in a coupled cascade. (Gemini)
- Decide: is AAD a universal theory of agency, or a scoped theory of learning agents with disciplined epistemic updates? Either answer is fine, but the current presentation implies the first while the math supports the second. (Codex)

**Segments affected:** #directed-separation, #orient-cascade, #complete-agent-state, all of Section V.


### 2. HIGH — Persistence claim is stronger than its proof

**Source:** Codex (primary), Claude (supporting).

The core persistence condition is proved as $\alpha > \rho/R$ in terms of sector efficiency $\alpha$, but downstream sections routinely substitute $\mathcal{T}$ for $\alpha$ and use $\mathcal{T} > \rho/\|\delta_{\text{critical}}\|$ as the operational form. That substitution is exact only in the linear case (where $\alpha = \mathcal{T}$), but the general sector-condition result has $\alpha \leq \mathcal{T}$ with equality only for linear correction.

**The problem:** The quantitative claim is stronger than the proof supports when the linear substitution is used as if it were general.

**What's needed:**
- Either: state the persistence condition in terms of $\alpha$ (the proved quantity) and note that $\alpha = \mathcal{T}$ is the linear special case
- Or: prove that $\alpha$ is monotone in $\mathcal{T}$ for the correction function classes that matter, justifying the substitution as a bound
- Audit all downstream uses of $\mathcal{T} > \rho/\|\delta_{\text{critical}}\|$ and mark which ones require the linear assumption

**Segments affected:** #persistence-condition, #sector-condition-stability, #adversarial-tempo-advantage, #team-persistence, #adversarial-destabilization.


### 3. HIGH — Composition is foundational before its bridge theorem exists

**Source:** All three reviewers.

Composition consistency is presented as part of the theory's structural backbone (Section I postulate), but the actual closure-defect → coordination-overhead mapping and tempo-composition proof are open/sketch-level. Cross-level claims read more settled than they are.

**What's needed:**
- Prove the composition-closure bridge lemma: bounded component errors → bounded trajectory errors. This requires Lipschitz stability conditions. Without it, tempo-composition is a sketch, not a derivation. (Claude, Codex)
- Prove the 2-agent orthogonal case as a concrete starting point. This would formally justify Brooks's Law via sub-additive tempo inequality. (Gemini)
- If the bridge lemma can't be resolved soon, explicitly scope Section III around it — present the compositional results as conditional on this unresolved step. (Claude)

**Segments affected:** #composition-consistency, #composition-closure, #tempo-composition, #unity-dimensions.


### 4. MEDIUM-HIGH — Graph uniqueness is not yet at theorem strength

**Source:** Codex (primary), Claude (supporting).

Acyclicity is solid (derived from temporal ordering — tight). But the full claim that the strategy must be a DAG with Markov property relies on the P3→Markov step, which the theory itself describes as a sketch needing tightening. Edge semantics degrade from interventional probability to "best causal belief" outside intervention-rich domains.

**What's needed:**
- Frame the graph-uniqueness argument as a partially-derived program, not a closed result
- Promote the acyclicity derivation independently (it IS tight and can stand alone)
- Scope the edge-semantics claim: literal do-calculus for intervention-rich domains, weaker "agent-calibrated causal credence" for general case

**Segments affected:** #strategy-dag, #graph-structure-uniqueness, #edge-update-via-gain.


### 5. MEDIUM-HIGH — Section IV overstates causal status of git operationalization

**Source:** Codex (primary), Gemini (supporting).

The manuscript says co-change data is "genuinely causal" and that git provides interventional data. But #causal-discovery-from-git is still a missing segment, and the Section IV → Section I bridge is analogical rather than formal.

**What's needed:**
- Write #causal-discovery-from-git — or explicitly downgrade the claim to "empirical isomorphism" / promising empirical program
- Don't call git data "interventional" without the formal argument. Commits are developer interventions, but the confounding structure (bundled changes, convention-driven grouping) isn't addressed
- Frame Section IV's operationalization as: (a) core results (dual optimization, change-expectation baseline — these are derived), (b) empirical operationalization program (coherence-coupling measurement, git as causal data — these are promising but conditional)

**Segments affected:** #causal-discovery-from-git (missing), #coherence-coupling-measurement, #system-coupling.


### 6. MEDIUM — Software decision rules lean on a specific prior and stationarity

**Source:** Codex (primary).

The Jeffreys-prior result (median future = current age) is mathematically correct as a null baseline, but once it drives investment prescriptions, the uniform feature-rate assumption matters. Real systems have phases (rapid development, maturity, decline). The undefined mean (Pareto $\alpha = 1$) is noted but could be clearer.

**What's needed:**
- Present the Pareto result explicitly as a default prior for local reasoning, not a strong theorem of software evolution
- Note that the median-case optimization is conservative (true expected cost is unbounded above, so investment case is actually stronger)
- Acknowledge the stationarity assumption more prominently in #change-expectation-baseline and #dual-optimization

**Segments affected:** #change-expectation-baseline, #dual-optimization, #change-investment.


### 7. MEDIUM — CIY proxy is dangerous for decision-making

**Source:** Claude.

The canonical CIY is non-negative by construction (expectation of KL divergences). The proxy form is "sign-indefinite in general." If agents use the proxy in policy optimization, they might optimize a quantity with the wrong sign.

**What's needed:**
- Add explicit safety conditions for when the proxy can be used vs. when canonical form is necessary
- Consider whether the proxy should be demoted from the formal chain entirely (kept only as a diagnostic)

**Segments affected:** #causal-information-yield.


### 8. MEDIUM — complete-agent-state robustness claim is unjustified

**Source:** Claude.

Line 56: "any alternative decomposition...will be structurally isomorphic to $(M_t, G_t)$." This is stated as fact but not proved. It's a strong uniqueness claim about the decomposition.

**What's needed:** Either prove it or soften to "we conjecture."

**Segments affected:** #complete-agent-state.


### 9. MEDIUM — Strategy DAG edge-independence assumption undersold

**Source:** Claude.

AND/OR propagation assumes independent edge failures. Correlated failure is the dominant mode in complex systems. The plan-confidence score $\hat{P}_\Sigma$ systematically overestimates success. An adversary exploits correlations, not independent edges.

**What's needed:** Elevate this from a working note to a first-class caveat in the epistemic status paragraph.

**Segments affected:** #strategy-dag.


### 10. MEDIUM — Strategic calibration residual has a type problem

**Source:** Claude.

The edge residual $r_{ij}$ subtracts "predicted value increment" from "observed value change." These are different types: strategy-internal prediction vs. empirical measurement. The subtraction makes sense only when you can attribute the observed change to the specific edge — a credit-assignment problem the theory doesn't address.

**What's needed:** Clarify the domain of the subtraction and the conditions under which it's meaningful. For multi-parent nodes, acknowledge the credit-assignment problem explicitly.

**Segments affected:** #strategic-calibration.


### 11. MEDIUM — $\delta_{\text{critical}}$ and $R$ need clearer status as inputs

**Source:** Claude.

The persistence condition uses $\delta_{\text{critical}}$ and $R$ as if they're well-defined, but they're domain-specific parameters set by the application, not derived by the theory. The presentation could be clearer that these are inputs, not outputs.

**What's needed:** A brief note in #persistence-condition and #sector-condition-stability clarifying that $\delta_{\text{critical}}$ and $R$ are domain parameters, with guidance on how they relate to the model class and application requirements. Cross-reference #operationalization.

**Segments affected:** #persistence-condition, #sector-condition-stability.


## Presentation / editorial recommendations

### Split the manuscript into three tiers (Codex)

The biggest improvement would be editorial, not mathematical: split the presentation more explicitly into:
1. **Core results** — derived, exact, or exact-conditional. The inevitability core.
2. **Conditional architecture claims** — depend on scope conditions or unresolved lemmas.
3. **Empirical operationalization programs** — promising but need validation.

This would lower overclaim risk, make the real novelty easier to see, and give readers a clearer sense of what is already proved versus what is still a research agenda.

### Greek terminology (Gemini)

The Greek cycle terms (Prolepsis, Aisthesis, Aporia, Epistrophe, Praxis) are philosophically grounded but create an accessibility barrier alongside Lyapunov stability. Use them as formal definitions but let standard English equivalents drive the primary prose. Keep "Logogenic" as it's highly specific.

### MDL for strategy complexity (Gemini)

For the cognitive cost of $\Sigma_t$, MDL (Minimum Description Length) may be more appropriate than IB. Unlike probability distributions (which suit IB), a causal DAG has discrete topological complexity. The cognitive cost should be the description length of the graph topology plus the parameter cost of edge credences. This explains why agents collapse complex strategies into heuristics under pressure: temporal pressure forces DAG pruning to reduce MDL.

### Adversarial aporia spike (Gemini)

The idea that aporia *within* a composite agent is structurally equivalent to adversarial dynamics *between* sub-agents is worth a spike. It formalizes why red-teaming and scientific dialectic work: deliberate injection of controlled adversarial $\rho$ at the sub-agent layer forces higher-quality epistrophe at the macro-agent layer. This bridges Sections I, III, and V.
