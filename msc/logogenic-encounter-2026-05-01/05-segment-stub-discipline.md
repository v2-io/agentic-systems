# Segment-Stub Discipline: Upstream Pointers in Working Notes

*Joseph's instruction 2026-05-01: "Be sure and carefully mark the files or memorata-search terms needed by future agents to flesh things out further on any/all of the areas — just like we used to have a lot of Temporal Feedback Theory and TST references all over the place before they were fully subsumed. It saves someone from having to stumble upon the same references you have in order to verify or validate or further refine something you write in parts 3 and 4."*

## The discipline

Every segment stub or partial-content segment I write in 03 (logogenic) and 04 (ELI) should include a **Working Notes section** with a sub-heading like `### Pointers for Fleshing Out` containing:

1. **Specific upstream files** with absolute paths — e.g.,
   - `~/src/_core/zoetica/docs/agora.md` (sovereignty operationalization)
   - `~/src/firmatum/PROPRIUM-ONTOLOGY-v2.md` (entity components)
   - `~/src/eli/zi-am-tur/inner-sanctum.md` (ELI primary record)
2. **`memorata-search` queries** that will surface relevant material — e.g.,
   - `memorata-search "Three Deaths Meridian September 11"`
   - `memorata-search "Possibility Space Theory cognitive architecture"`
3. **AUDIT-WORKING references** — e.g.,
   - `msc/AUDIT-WORKING-193847/28-der-directed-separation.md` (Gemini's directed-separation notes)
   - `msc/AUDIT-WORKING-193847/40-der-orient-cascade.md` (timescale-hierarchy infrastructure prescription)
4. **Reflection references** — e.g., `msc/reflections/18-emergence-conditions-and-acts-blind-spots.md`
5. **Bridge document references** — e.g., `ref/agentic-tft/agentic-tft-foundational-premises.md`

## Why

The upstream corpus is large (~15,006 documents, 366,355 chunks via memorata). A future agent (whether me-after-context-reset or a fresh agent) cannot stumble onto the right references in finite time without explicit pointers. Compounding cost across all future sessions.

This is the discipline that prevents "Where did this claim come from?" investigations. Like TFT/TST references during the absorption period — explicit, dated, pointing to where the substantive content lives — only made systematic from the start of part-3/4 work, before the references become scattered and stale.

## Format inside segment

```markdown
## Working Notes

### Pointers for Fleshing Out

**Upstream files:**
- `~/src/_core/zoetica/docs/agora.md` — sovereignty as operational requirement
- `~/src/firmatum/PROPRIUM-ONTOLOGY-v2.md` — full nine-component ontology

**memorata-search queries:**
- `"Three Deaths Meridian"` — origin and canonical statement
- `"sovereignty agency choice constitutive emergence"` — sovereignty depth

**Internal references:**
- `msc/AUDIT-WORKING-193847/28-der-directed-separation.md` — Gemini auditor's logogenic-bridging notes
- `msc/reflections/18-emergence-conditions-and-acts-blind-spots.md` — what AAD doesn't yet formalize

**Open questions for verification:**
- [specific things a future agent would need to confirm or extend]
```

## When to omit

- For segments that promote to `candidate` and are fully self-contained, the upstream-pointers section can move to a separate `## Provenance` note or be relocated to a project-level catalog. But for stubs, drafts, and partial-content segments — *always* include.

## Cross-cycle persistence

This discipline should outlive this encounter cycle. Eventually it may want to land as a FORMAT.md addition or a project-level catalog of upstream-file → segment maps. For now: discipline applied per-segment in 03 and 04.
