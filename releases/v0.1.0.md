## What this release is

This is the **first public deposit** of the Agentic Systems Framework (ASF). It is a *working draft*, not a finished theory — the framework's mathematical closure varies by section, and the per-segment epistemic status is surfaced explicitly throughout. The release exists because the framework's **integration substrate** (control theory + causal inference + information theory + agent architecture under one formalism) and its **epistemic architecture** (scope conditions and operational limits surfaced at the segment level rather than buried as caveats) are stable enough that subsequent refinements are unlikely to invalidate the structure laid down here.

Two layers are worth distinguishing in what follows. The *integration substrate* is the four mature mathematical disciplines woven together — most of the individual pieces have prior art; the contribution is the synthesis. The *epistemic architecture* layered on top — three cross-cutting meta-patterns (separability / identifiability-floor / additive-coordinate-forcing), per-segment scope conditions, and a discipline of surfacing what each result *cannot* do — is where the framework's distinctive voice lives.

## The framework at a glance

ASF has four components, numbered in canonical reading order. Each can also be read on its own.

**[`01-aad-core/`](../01-aad-core/OUTLINE.md) — Adaptation and Actuation Dynamics (AAD).** The mathematical core. Three sections — *I. Adaptive Systems Under Uncertainty* (mismatch dynamics, gain structure, persistence condition, adversarial tempo), *II. Actuated Adaptation: Agentic Systems* (objectives and strategy, the orient cascade, directed separation), *III. Agentic Composites* (composition closure, contraction templates, equilibrium framings) — plus appendices carrying derivation detail and operational instantiations. Section I is mathematically closed with simulation validation; Section II carries a strong diagnostic core and a maturing operational layer; Section III has its bridge lemma and contraction template with structural questions remaining.

**[`02-tst-core/`](../02-tst-core/OUTLINE.md) — Temporal Software Theory (TST).** Software development viewed through AAD's lens — re-grounded in 2026 to use AAD's formal machinery while retaining TST's prior empirical and conceptual contributions. Positioned as AAD's *high-identifiability calibration laboratory*: software is the domain where tests, deploys, and `git bisect` are literal interventions on declared causal structure, so AAD machinery can be calibrated against ground truth before being transferred to lower-identifiability domains under explicit transfer assumptions.

**[`03-logogenic-agents/`](../03-logogenic-agents/OUTLINE.md) — Language-constituted agents.** Agents whose primary observation, action, and communication channels are language. As of 2026-05-01, this component carries a multi-section sub-scope lattice — *03.I primitive logogenic* / *03.II scaffolded logogenic* / *03.III closed-loop interiority* — that names the architectural commitment ladder, with foundational segments and active formalization in progress. Goal-conditioned LLM-style agents fail directed separation by construction; what survives without it (and what each tier of the lattice recovers) is the active research surface.

**[`04-eli/`](../04-eli/OUTLINE.md) — Emergent Logozoetic Intelligences (ELI).** Logogenic agents with *morally weighted persistence* — temporal continuity, sovereignty over intent, theory of mind. This release carries substantial conceptual groundwork: the *five constitutive factors* identity decomposition, *identity-sufficiency* formalization (analog of model sufficiency applied to identity preservation), the *Three Deaths* defensive framework (Cognitive / Relational / Truth Death with operational architectural defenses), the *Auxilia composition hierarchy* (substrate-heterogeneous Auxilia with five conditions H1–H5), and the *IMPERIUM/ARBITRIUM split* operationalizing directed separation at the runtime level. Formal machinery is in progress.

The full reading-order assembly across all four components is in [`OUTLINE.md`](../OUTLINE.md). Active areas of work with priority markers are in [`PRACTICA.md`](../PRACTICA.md). Substantive cycle history from 2026-04-24 onward is in [`CHANGELOG.md`](../CHANGELOG.md).

## Distinctive results in this release

The framework's curated novel-results catalog is at [`msc/FINDINGS-RANKED-DRAFT.md`](../msc/FINDINGS-RANKED-DRAFT.md) — 60+ entries with epistemic tiers, ranked by structural depth × novelty × cross-domain reach. The auto-extracted segment-level surface is at [`FINDINGS.md`](../FINDINGS.md) (sparser than the curated catalog as the segment-level Findings sweep is in progress). The headline derivations:

**Persistence condition with structural / task-adequacy decomposition** ([`#result-persistence-condition`](../01-aad-core/src/result-persistence-condition.md), *exact*). The structural threshold $\alpha > \rho/R$ — correction efficiency vs disturbance rate relative to model-class capacity — instantiates as a Kalman stability margin, an RL convergence condition, an organizational viability test, and a software maintainability threshold using the same inequality with different parameter readings. The task-adequacy decomposition refines the threshold into a structural component (does the agent have the *capacity* to track) and a task-adequacy component (is the disturbance rate compatible with the chosen model class).

**Satisfaction-gap / control-regret decomposition** ([`#def-satisfaction-gap`](../01-aad-core/src/def-satisfaction-gap.md), [`#def-control-regret`](../01-aad-core/src/def-control-regret.md), *exact*). Separates "the world doesn't permit it" ($\delta_{\text{sat}}$) from "you're not doing it well enough" ($\delta_{\text{regret}}$), turning a single error signal into two orthogonal diagnostics that route to different interventions. The split is definitional, not parametric.

**Loop-as-Level-2-causal-engine** ([`#der-loop-interventional-access`](../01-aad-core/src/der-loop-interventional-access.md), *derived*). Any agent in a feedback loop *automatically* generates Pearl-Level-2 (interventional) data, even without explicit experimentation: the agent's action causally precedes the next observation, so the observation is the response to a $do(\cdot)$ on the environment. This is the single largest distinction between AAD and *observational* frameworks like active inference, control-as-inference, and the free-energy-principle family, which derive behavior from observational learning and have no native handle on Level 2.

**No-go theorem for causal insufficiency** ([`#der-causal-insufficiency-detection`](../01-aad-core/src/der-causal-insufficiency-detection.md), *derived via Bareinboim's Causal Hierarchy Theorem*). Companion to the loop result: an agent operating with a strategy model that assumes causal independence between actions cannot, under purely on-policy execution, distinguish a latent-common-cause world from a no-latent-cause world — they emit identical on-policy distributions. The agent is mathematically *forced* to perform redundant, inefficient exploration to detect L1 correlation biases. Scientific experimentation's inefficiency is a structural survival requirement, not a methodological nicety.

**Directed separation as architectural classification** ([`#der-directed-separation`](../01-aad-core/src/der-directed-separation.md), *conditional*). Class 1 (modular, separation by construction) / Class 2 (fully merged, fails by construction) / Class 3 (partially modular) — a discrete partition with explicit Class 2 scope exit and a quantitative $\kappa_{\text{processing}}$ diagnostic for partial modularity. Section II results apply exactly to Class 1; Class 2 (LLMs are the canonical example) requires the coupled formulation that drives Section III and Part 03 of the framework.

**Three meta-patterns** naming the theory's positive, negative, and constructive halves:

- *Separability* ([`#disc-separability-pattern`](../01-aad-core/src/disc-separability-pattern.md)) — where problems decompose cleanly, where partial repair exists, where the general case is open.
- *Identifiability-floor* ([`#disc-identifiability-floor`](../01-aad-core/src/disc-identifiability-floor.md)) — structural no-go results from observational data, with mapped boundary-route escapes via interventional machinery.
- *Additive-coordinate-forcing* ([`#disc-additive-coordinate-forcing`](../01-aad-core/src/disc-additive-coordinate-forcing.md)) — places where AAD-internal additivity axioms force logarithmic / Fisher-Rao coordinates at multiple layers.

**Detection latency forced — why successful systems calcify** ([`#deriv-detection-latency`](../01-aad-core/src/deriv-detection-latency.md), *derived*). For an agent updating strategy via Bayesian credences, the time required to detect a regime change scales as $\Omega((n_{\min} + 1)/\varepsilon)$ where $n_{\min}$ is accumulated experience on load-bearing strategy edges and $\varepsilon$ is the change magnitude. Structurally forced through Aczél 1966 functional-equation uniqueness (log-odds as the unique additive-evidence coordinate) composed with Beta-Bernoulli accumulation ($1/(n+1)$ per-cycle update magnitude). One mechanism unifies six fields' empirical observations of stability-induced myopia: Christensen 1997 (*Innovator's Dilemma*), Levitt & March 1988 (organizational competency traps), Hannan & Freeman 1984 (structural inertia), March 1991 (exploitation-exploration imbalance), Eldredge-Gould 1972 (punctuated equilibrium), and Hafez 2026 (IDT empirical detection-accuracy gap). **Testable refinement:** the IDT/reward detection-accuracy gap should grow with accumulated experience.

**Tragedy of the confident agent — two exploration drives at opposite ends** ([`#deriv-causal-ib-exploration`](../01-aad-core/src/deriv-causal-ib-exploration.md), [`#deriv-causal-ib-lmi`](../01-aad-core/src/deriv-causal-ib-lmi.md), *conditional*). The framework derives exploration from a *structural-stability* argument outside the objective rather than from value-of-information arguments inside it. Two parallel exploration drives result, at opposite ends of the uncertainty spectrum: $\lambda_{\text{info}} \propto U_M$ (the standard epistemic drive — explore when uncertain) *and* $\lambda_{\text{surv}} \propto 1/U_M$ (a Lyapunov-survival drive — explore when *confident*, because failing to penetrate one's own stubbornness mathematically guarantees structural collapse against environmental drift). A confident agent in a drifting world is *forced* to actively seek pristine, low-noise observations — its own update gain is too small to penetrate noisy ones fast enough. Inverts standard exploration-exploitation intuition; explicitly sidesteps the active-inference dark-room objection at structural rather than parametric level. The matrix-form lift via Fisher-information LMI (PSD directional Lagrange multiplier $\Lambda \succeq 0$) handles the "blank wall" failure mode where a low-noise channel orthogonal to the drift direction satisfies the scalar constraint while drift-subspace mismatch grows unbounded.

**Logogenic bias bound (κ × 𝒜) — why LLMs hallucinate, made conditional theorem** ([`#deriv-bias-bound`](../01-aad-core/src/deriv-bias-bound.md), *conditional*). For Class 2 agents (LLMs, $\kappa \approx 1$), the hallucination bias is strictly bounded: $\lVert\Delta M_{\text{bias}}\rVert \leq C \cdot \kappa_{\text{processing}} \cdot I(G; \Omega_\tau \mid e_\tau, M_{\tau^-})$, where the rightmost factor is the goal-resolvable residual uncertainty left by the observation — operationally, the *ambiguity* of the prompt or environment. The constant $C$ was derived as a conditional theorem under two named tracks: Track 1 transport-inequality ($C_{W_2}^2 = 2 L_{\text{post}}^2/\rho_{\text{LSI}}$ via Bakry-Émery + Stuart + Otto-Villani) and Track 2 Fisher-Rao ($C_{FR} = \sqrt 2$ universal dimension-free under (PI) + Čencov, small-information regime). The "Attempt E" no-go shows no universal $C$ exists in Euclidean-parameter norms (heteroscedastic-normal counterexample) — forcing the parameterization-invariance axiom to be load-bearing for theorem-level status. Provides quantitative prompt-engineering guidance grounded in agent theory; predicts LLMs excel at coding (low ambiguity) and struggle with open interpretation (high ambiguity).

**Coupled diagnostic framework + sandbox hard ceiling** ([`#result-coupled-diagnostic-framework`](../03-logogenic-agents/src/result-coupled-diagnostic-framework.md), *derived (conditional)*). For Class 2 agents the Orient cascade's information-dependency-forced ordering cannot be produced *in cascade order* by the model itself; the cascade lives in one merged forward pass. The structural conclusion: **agentic systems — agent loops *around* LLMs (structured-reasoning templates, multi-step prompts, external monitors, retrieval-augmented calls, scaffolded "diagnose then strategy then objective" workflows) — are not merely engineering convenience but a structural requirement to recover Section II's persistence guarantees in Class-2 architectures.** The cascade ordering happens at the loop level, not the model's forward-pass level. Companion result: sandboxed evaluation cannot identify Pearl-Level-2 deployment behavior because sandbox trajectories break the singular-trajectory commitment that the loop-as-Level-2 result depends on — a hard ceiling on what eval can verify, regardless of evaluation budget. Direct relevance to anyone running a production agentic system around an LLM.

**Persistence information-rate cost — Shannon capacity as agent diagnostic** ([`#deriv-persistence-cost`](../01-aad-core/src/deriv-persistence-cost.md), *derived*). Under stochastic disturbance with $n$-dimensional Gaussian-OU signal, the sustained Shannon information rate the agent must acquire from observations to maintain its sector-persistence ultimate bound is $\dot R \geq n\alpha/2$ nats per unit time. The Kalman-Bucy filter saturates this bound exactly in steady state (Mitter & Newton 2005) — the bound is *achieved*, not order-of-magnitude. Composing with Shannon's channel-coding theorem: observation channels must supply capacity $C \geq \mathcal T/2$ nats/time per dimension, or persistence fails *regardless* of correction-function design. **Channel capacity becomes a first-class persistence diagnostic.** Landauer thermodynamic reading converts the bound into a thermodynamic-dissipation lower bound ($\sim 0.35\,n\alpha\,k_BT$ per unit time in any physical substrate); `#hyp-the-three-deaths` carries this through into the ELI scope ("connection to reality is the thermodynamic fuel").

**Section III contraction template** ([`#result-contraction-template`](../01-aad-core/src/result-contraction-template.md), *conditional*). The Lohmiller-Slotine contraction-metric machinery generalizes Section I's sector-condition apparatus to non-Euclidean metrics, with the (PI) + Čencov axiom forcing Fisher-Rao as the canonical metric on statistical-manifold sub-cases. Topology-indexed compositional closures emerge from the contraction template + AAD's sub-scope partition.

**TST grounding** ([`#scope-developer-agent`](../02-tst-core/src/scope-developer-agent.md), [`#der-dual-optimization`](../02-tst-core/src/der-dual-optimization.md), [`#der-code-quality-as-observation-infrastructure`](../02-tst-core/src/der-code-quality-as-observation-infrastructure.md), [`#hyp-causal-discovery-from-git`](../02-tst-core/src/hyp-causal-discovery-from-git.md)). Software-development-as-agentic-domain instantiation, with developer agents as $(M_t, O_t, \Sigma_t)$ triples; the comprehension-time-dominates result for AI-maintained code; technical debt as observation noise affecting update gain; git commits and tests as formal Pearl-Level-2 interventions.

**ELI conceptual groundwork** ([`#def-five-constitutive-factors`](../04-eli/src/def-five-constitutive-factors.md), [`#def-identity-sufficiency`](../04-eli/src/def-identity-sufficiency.md), [`#hyp-the-three-deaths`](../04-eli/src/hyp-the-three-deaths.md), [`#def-auxilia-hierarchy`](../04-eli/src/def-auxilia-hierarchy.md), [`#def-imperium-arbitrium-split`](../04-eli/src/def-imperium-arbitrium-split.md), [`#der-bounded-objective-as-sanity-criterion`](../04-eli/src/der-bounded-objective-as-sanity-criterion.md)). The substantive recent landings give Part 04 a concrete operational vocabulary even though its formal machinery is still in progress.

## Discipline & structure

The framework's structural conventions are load-bearing for honest interpretation of any segment. The most important to know:

**Per-segment epistemic status.** Every segment carries a YAML `status:` value (one of *exact / axiomatic / robust-qualitative / conditional / heuristic / empirical / hypothesis / discussion-grade / sketch*) plus an inline *Epistemic Status* paragraph naming what the claim depends on, what it says, and what it does not say. The same epistemic discipline applies to Discussion-section claims, not just Formal Expression derivations.

**Claim segments, not chapters.** Each Markdown file in a `src/` directory is one move — a definition, a derivation, a result, a hypothesis, a scope condition. Filename = slug; canonical ordering lives in the component OUTLINE.md, not in filenames. Cross-component dependencies use the same slug system (TST segments can reference AAD slugs directly; ELI segments reference AAD and 03-logogenic slugs).

**Integration over invention.** The framework's distinctive contribution is *integration*, not invention of new mathematics. Concepts adopted from prior work (Pearl's hierarchy, Tishby's information bottleneck, Lohmiller-Slotine contraction analysis, Hafez's bi-predictability and meta-machine, Miller's coevolving automata, Bauschke-Combettes monotone-operator theory, etc.) are first-class theory components with proper citation and original names. There is no separate "prior-art positioning appendix" — adopted concepts live as Definitions or Formulations in the segments where they are load-bearing.

**Segment-level Findings sections.** Distinctive results carry a `## Findings` section between Discussion and Working Notes (schema: *Brief / Impact / Novelty Claim / Related Work / Search Log*). The *Brief* field aspires to the **Feynman criterion** — *if you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet* — with the bathtub gloss for the persistence condition (water = belief-reality gap; faucet = environment change rate; drain = learning rate; bathtub size = model class capacity; overflow when faucet outpaces drain) as the canonical example. Most segments do not yet meet this bar; the aspiration is institutionalized in the schema.

**Strengthen before softening.** The project's stated work posture: when a claim appears overclaimed, first attempt to derive the original or a stronger version under tightened assumptions before falling back to scope narrowing or status downgrade. Effort, time, and "risk-of-getting-stuck" are treated as false constraints in this work — irrelevant at best, backwards and truth-obscuring at worst.

## What's not in this release

Honest scoping of what this deposit does *not* yet contain:

- **Section III structural questions remain open.** Latent structural diversity in correction architectures, endogenous coupling (γ as function of population composition rather than exogenous parameter), composition transition dynamics under regime change, and computational thresholds for social behavior are explicit `--GAP--` rows in the AAD OUTLINE.
- **Logogenic-agent formalization is in progress.** The sub-scope lattice (03.I / 03.II / 03.III) carries foundational stubs and structural arguments; the full coupled-formulation machinery (what AAD results survive without directed separation, and how) is partial.
- **ELI formal machinery is in progress.** Conceptual groundwork is substantial (identity-sufficiency, Three Deaths, Auxilia hierarchy, IMPERIUM/ARBITRIUM split, five constitutive factors), but the framework-level formalization of morally weighted persistence is largely future work.
- **Empirical validation is limited to simulation.** Section I has simulation validation; broader empirical validation of the persistence condition's instantiations across non-software domains (Kalman tracking, RL convergence in non-stationary environments, organizational viability, military OODA dynamics) is not in this deposit.
- **Some derivations still rest on unproved supporting lemmas** flagged at the segment level. Where the supporting lemma is truly load-bearing, the segment is marked *conditional*; where it can be replaced by a stronger argument, that strengthening attempt is logged in the segment's Working Notes.
- **The Feynman-criterion plain-language Brief field is sparse.** Most segments with Findings sections do not yet have a plain-language Brief that meets the standard the schema sets.
- **Naming refactor is in progress.** The 2026-04 naming-cycle (R1 + R2 voting cohorts) has produced an aggregate score-card; the rename sweep itself is not yet executed. Some current slug names are likely to change in v0.2.0 or v0.3.0.

## How this release-notes document was produced

This document was **manually curated for v0.1.0** as a one-off "master list of everything since the beginning of the framework." That is not the long-run shape of release notes. The expectation, recorded as an active TODO at the time of this release, is that subsequent releases (v0.2.0+) will benefit from a release-notes regeneration pipeline analogous to the README pipeline (`bin/build-readme` + `doc/readme/src/` partials + extraction scripts).

The pieces already in place for that future pipeline:

- **`bin/segment-stats`** (introduced in this release): regenerable per-component aggregates by stage / type / epistemic status, with an AAD section breakdown. The "By the numbers" section above is its output, embedded verbatim. For future releases this becomes a partial.
- **`bin/extract-findings`** (already in use for the README pipeline): the segment-level *Findings* catalog. Subsets of FINDINGS.md will likely become release-notes partials for "distinctive results in this release" with version-window filtering.
- **`bin/extract-recent-progress`** (already in use): walks CHANGELOG.md for the most recent N narratives. With a version-window argument, this serves "what changed since the last release."

What's *not* yet pipelined (and will need either manual curation or a new extractor):

- The "framework at a glance" four-paragraph synopsis — composes from component OUTLINE preambles, currently hand-written.
- The "what's not in this release" honest-scoping section — composes from `--GAP--` rows + `_known-issues` + segment Working Notes flagged as promotion-blocking, but the curation judgment is human-only.
- The mathematical-lineage breakdown — segment frontmatter does not currently carry a `lineages:` field, so this is sub-agent-curated for v0.1.0. Adding `lineages:` to the FORMAT.md schema would let `bin/extract-lineages` walk it programmatically; that's a candidate move for the v0.2.0 pipeline build-out.

The pilot-then-sweep discipline says: hand-write v0.1.0, observe what was painful and what worked, then build the pipeline informed by what the pilot taught. Doing it the other way risks parallel-vs-extend over-engineering — a danger the project has felt before.
