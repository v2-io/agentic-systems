## Where the Reviews Converge

Both reviews independently reached the same structural assessment:

1. **Section I is mathematically closed.** The recursive-update derivation, sector-condition
   Lyapunov proofs, and persistence condition form a coherent, rigorous chain. The Kalman
   worked example validates end-to-end. This is publishable-quality work.

2. **Section II has a strong diagnostic core and an incomplete operational layer.** The
   orient cascade's *ordering* is exact (forced by information dependency), but its
   *content* for strategy maintenance (steps 3–5) rests on three unresolved pieces: an
   undefined signal function, unresolved credit assignment, and a strategy-persistence
   schema that is explicitly a promissory note.

3. **Section III is a promising scaffold, not yet a closed theory.** The bridge lemma
   connecting closure defect to trajectory error is the strongest result, but projection
   admissibility is undefined, load-bearing norms are unspecified, and the macro-agent is
   currently "a not-yet-fully-defined object."

4. **Epistemic calibration is extraordinary.** Both reviews noted that the segment-level
   self-assessment (exact / conditional / discussion-grade / sketch) almost perfectly matches
   what the segments actually demonstrate. This is the theory's most distinctive quality.

## Directed Separation: Detailed Scope Analysis

The initial Opus review stated this forcefully: "an exact theory of a regime that didn't need
a new theory, and a promissory note for the regime that does." This framing was too strong.
The situation is more nuanced.

### What directed separation actually buys

The #der-directed-separation segment itself states (line 96): Section I quantities (δ, η*, T,
persistence condition) remain well-defined on M_t regardless of whether directed separation
holds. What directed separation provides is the *clean factorized update*: M_t updates
independently, then G_t updates in light of the new M_t, and the orient cascade resolves
sequentially.

### Results that survive for Class 2 (fully merged) agents

**Satisfaction gap and control regret** — defined in terms of V_O, A_O, π_current. None
reference f_M or f_G. The 2×2 diagnostic works for any agent with an objective and a policy.

**Orient cascade ordering** — the information dependency (what's true → strategy evaluation →
action) exists even in coupled systems. The cascade becomes a simultaneous fixed-point
problem rather than a sequential resolution, but the logical dependency structure is
unchanged.

**Strategy DAG structure** — goals decompose into subgoals causally regardless of whether
perception is goal-conditioned. Acyclicity still holds. Observability dominance still holds
(gain goes to zero for unobservable edges regardless of architecture).

**All Section I quantities** — δ, η*, T, the persistence condition, adaptive reserve, the
sector-condition Lyapunov machinery. These are defined on M_t without reference to G_t.

### Results that genuinely fail for Class 2

**Sequential M-then-G update** — no well-defined moment where M_t is updated but G_t is not.
The two substates are entangled in the processing architecture.

**G_t complexity bound** — depends on M_t being independently established. Coupled processing
allows the agent to "see what it wants to see."

**Clean persistence analysis on M_t alone** — mismatch dynamics become a coupled system where
perception quality depends on goals.

### Results where it's genuinely unclear

**Persistence condition form.** The Lyapunov machinery should still yield α > ρ/R, but α
would now depend on G_t (goal-conditioned perception changes correction quality). This makes
the persistence condition *state-dependent* rather than purely *structural* — a meaningful
qualitative change. The three-sense persistence taxonomy in LEXICON.md (structural /
operational / continuity) was partly motivated by this observation: for coupled agents, the
line between structural and operational persistence blurs. The correction machinery's
capacity is no longer a fixed property of the architecture; it varies with the agent's
current goals and the interaction between goals and perception.

**Adversarial dynamics.** Goal-conditioned perception opens a new attack surface: an adversary
who knows the target's goals can feed observations that exploit motivated reasoning. This has
no analog for Class 1 agents. The adversarial exponents might change.

### Revised assessment

More accurately than the initial framing:

- Section I applies to all agents without qualification
- Section II's *diagnostic vocabulary* (satisfaction gap, control regret, orient cascade
  ordering, observability dominance) applies to all agents
- Section II's *factorized dynamics* (sequential M→G update, persistence on M_t alone)
  apply only to Class 1
- The theory's *classification of the failure mode* (κ_processing, three architectural
  classes) applies to all agents and is itself a novel contribution
- The *engineering guidance* (design LLM agent systems with modular topology to recover
  approximate separation) is arguably the most practically valuable consequence for the
  primary target application

What's missing is a formal treatment of what happens to the dynamics when separation fails —
the coupled formulation. Not just "it's harder" but: what new phenomena emerge (motivated
reasoning, confirmation bias as coupling terms in f_M), what the persistence condition
becomes for coupled systems, and which results degrade gracefully vs. break entirely. This is
the scope of 03-logogenic-agents/.


## The Framework-vs-First-Principles Tension

Both reviews converged on the characterization: "AAD is a unifying framework with specific
novel results at the joints, not a new mathematical theory from first principles." This is
accurate as a snapshot, but the theory's own development history shows the boundary is
unstable:

- **Acyclicity** was assumed in early versions, then derived from temporal ordering. What was
  a framework decision became a first-principles result.
- **The α/T relationship** appeared to be a simple substitution, then turned out to require
  per-correction-function-class verification — a genuine mathematical investigation that
  revealed structure.
- **The P3→Markov step** may follow the same trajectory: currently conditional on causal
  sufficiency, but the three-part argument (locality, directionality, sufficiency from
  agent-constructed Σ_t) suggests a tighter derivation may exist.
- **AND/OR restriction** converged independently across three formalism attempts. If a
  parsimony theorem can be established (AND/OR as the unique minimal complete basis for
  strategy nodes under the theory's constraints), this moves from framework choice to forced
  structure.

The pattern is: what looks like a framework decision sometimes turns out to be the only
option compatible with the prior objects. The epistemic triage questions in FORMAT.md are
designed to detect this ("what competing formulation would also fit?"), but the answer can
change as more structure is discovered.

**Implication for presentation:** Rather than committing to "framework" or "first
principles," the theory should explicitly track which claims are currently framework choices
with plausible derivation paths. The genuinely novel results (persistence condition,
acyclicity derivation, satisfaction/regret split, adversarial exponents, bridge lemma) can be
stated independently of the framework decisions. The framework decisions that have resisted
all alternative formulations (AND/OR convergence, the orient cascade ordering) deserve a
status between "choice" and "derived" — perhaps something like "convergent" or
"operationally unique."


## Proposed Spikes

### Spike 1: Single-Edge Strategic Dynamics (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Goal:** Close the strategy-maintenance gap with the simplest possible concrete case.

**Setup:** One agent, one strategy edge, binary outcomes (success/fail), Beta-Bernoulli
updating. This is the segment's own suggested first step (strategy-persistence-schema
Working Notes).

**What it would establish:**
- Verify the sector condition for the Beta-Bernoulli correction function (does the update
  point "inward" with lower-bounded efficiency?)
- Derive the signal function for the binary case (straightforward: observe success or failure
  of the predicted causal link)
- Compute δ_strategic exactly (single edge, no credit assignment needed)
- Determine whether the persistence threshold has the same α > ρ/R form or differs

**Why it matters:** This is the minimum viable instantiation of the strategy-maintenance
loop. If the sector condition holds for even this toy case, the schema promotes from "sketch"
to "conditional." If it fails, that's important information about whether the Lyapunov
machinery extends to strategy dynamics at all.

**Estimated scope:** A few pages. The Beta distribution's update dynamics are well-understood;
the main work is verifying the sector condition and deriving the persistence threshold.

### Spike 2: Disturbance Model Split (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Goal:** Formalize the deterministic-drift vs. stochastic-noise distinction and propagate
it through the core dynamics.

**Setup:** Starting from mismatch-dynamics, define two disturbance models:
- Deterministic: ‖w(t)‖ ≤ ρ_det (bounded, persistent)
- Stochastic: w(t) ~ distribution with scale σ_w (zero-mean, possibly heavy-tailed)

**What it would establish:**
- Steady-state mismatch: ρ_det/α (deterministic) vs. σ_w/√(2α) (stochastic) — different
  scaling in α
- Persistence threshold: α > ρ_det/R (deterministic) vs. different form (stochastic)
- Adversarial exponent: b = 2 (deterministic, coupling-dominant) vs. b = 3/2 (stochastic,
  coupling-dominant) — now derived rather than empirical
- Per-dimension persistence: resolves the regime-mixing issue (currently uses deterministic
  threshold notation with stochastic formal expression)

**Why it matters:** The ρ ambiguity currently propagates through persistence,
adversarial coupling, per-dimension analysis, and operationalization. Splitting it at the
source would clean up several downstream inconsistencies simultaneously. The codex review
correctly identifies this as the single most impactful structural clarification.

**Estimated scope:** Moderate. The mathematical content is standard (Itô vs. bounded
perturbation Lyapunov analysis). The main work is propagating the distinction through
downstream segments.

### Spike 3: Projection Admissibility for Composition (MEDIUM-HIGH)

**Goal:** Define the class of admissible projections Λ for composition closure, and
instantiate for a two-agent toy case.

**Setup:** The composition-closure bridge lemma takes an infimum over admissible projections
and macro-dynamics. The macro-dynamics admissibility (A1–A4) is specified. The projection
admissibility is not.

**What to investigate:**
- Information-preservation approach: Λ must retain sufficient predictive mutual information
  (composition-closure Working Notes suggest this)
- Lipschitz condition: the spike-composition-bridge-2agent already assumes projection is
  Lipschitz — can this be derived from information preservation?
- Norm choices: specify for at least one domain (e.g., two Kalman filters tracking correlated
  processes)

**Why it matters:** Without projection admissibility, the closure defect ε* is an infimum
over an undefined set, and all quantitative composition results are about an undefined
object. This is the most immediate formal gap in Section III.

**Estimated scope:** Moderate to large. The two-Kalman-filter case might yield exact results
(linear systems, Gaussian noise). The general case may remain formulation-level.

### Spike 4: Scalar Objective Scope Analysis (MEDIUM)

**Goal:** Determine whether the scalar V_O restriction is load-bearing or removable.

**Setup:** objective-functional explicitly restricts to scalar-valued V_O (total ordering on
trajectories). This propagates into satisfaction-gap and control-regret. The question is
whether the theory's diagnostic structure survives a vector-valued extension.

**What to investigate:**
- Can satisfaction gap be defined component-wise for vector V_O? (Probably yes, but the
  single-number diagnostic is lost.)
- Does control regret require a total ordering? (The supremum over policies requires
  comparing policies, which requires a partial order at minimum.)
- The Working Notes suggest modeling compound objectives as terminal AND-nodes in Σ_t with
  simple scalar O_t. Does this actually work for incommensurable objectives?
- Is the restriction an artifact of the formalism or a genuine theoretical insight (that
  effective agency requires commensurability)?

**Why it matters:** If the restriction is essential, the theory should say so clearly and
explain why. If it's removable, the extension should be sketched. Either way, the current
state (restriction acknowledged but not analyzed) leaves the diagnostic claims' universality
ambiguous.

**Estimated scope:** Small to moderate. Mostly conceptual analysis, not heavy derivation.

### Spike 5: External Validation Design (MEDIUM)

**Goal:** Design at least one empirical test of AAD predictions against real-world data.

**Why it matters:** Both reviews note that the simulation program validates internal
coherence but not external predictions. The theory makes specific quantitative predictions
(persistence threshold, adversarial exponent, weak-dimension bottleneck) that should be
testable.

**Candidates for first external test:**
- **Software teams:** Operationalize tempo, disturbance rate, and model class fitness for a
  development team using git data. Predict which teams/projects approach the persistence
  boundary. Compare to actual outcomes (project stalls, team dissolution, quality collapse).
  This is the TST bridge, but would validate AAD-level predictions.
- **RL agents:** The bandit worked example already maps AAD concepts to RL. Compare AAD's
  per-arm persistence predictions to actual arm-dropping behavior in deployed bandit systems.
- **Control systems:** Compare AAD's persistence condition to known stability margins in
  deployed adaptive controllers. The Kalman case is exact; do approximate cases (EKF,
  particle filters) show the predicted mismatch scaling?

**Estimated scope:** Large (design only in the spike; execution is a separate project).

## Discussion: What the Theory Actually Contributes

### The Novel Results (Can Be Stated Independently)

These are AAD's genuine mathematical contributions, independent of the framework:

1. **Persistence condition** (α > ρ/R): A single inequality governing adaptive system
   viability, derived from sector-condition Lyapunov analysis. Generalizes across correction
   function classes.

2. **Acyclicity from temporal ordering:** Strategy graphs over finite planning horizons must
   be acyclic. Derived, not assumed. This is clean and publishable on its own.

3. **Satisfaction gap / control regret split:** Decomposes the gap between desired and actual
   outcomes into "the world doesn't permit it" vs. "you're not doing it well enough." This
   is definitional but genuinely useful — it disambiguates four corrective-action regimes.

4. **Adversarial tempo exponents:** The regime-dependent scaling (b = 2, 3/2, ~1) is a
   concrete, testable prediction that emerged from simulation forcing theory revision. This
   is how good empirical science works.

5. **Composition bridge lemma:** Bounded closure defect implies bounded trajectory error
   under contraction. Connects the composition story to the same Lyapunov machinery as
   Section I.

6. **Orient cascade ordering:** The resolution order (epistemic → attainability → regret →
   calibration → objective revision) is forced by information dependency. Not a design
   recommendation but a mathematical consequence.

### The Framework Contributions (Vocabulary + Structure)

These are not derived results but representational choices that organize the space:

- X_t = (M_t, G_t) decomposition with directed separation as scope condition
- G_t = (O_t, Σ_t) evaluation/guidance split
- Strategy as probabilistic AND/OR DAG with single-parameter edges
- Adaptive tempo as speed × quality aggregation
- Unity dimensions for multi-agent analysis
- Shared intent as IB-optimal compression of purpose

These are valuable precisely because they provide a common language for connecting control
theory, causal inference, and agent architecture. The value is in the connections, not in any
single piece.

### The Convergent Choices (Framework Decisions That May Be Forced)

A category between "derived" and "chosen" — framework decisions where all investigated
alternatives fail or converge:

- **AND/OR node types:** Three independent formalism attempts converged here. Noisy-OR and
  WEIGHTED were rejected for principled reasons (parameter explosion, non-identifiability).
  A parsimony theorem would promote this to "derived."
- **Single-parameter edges:** Same convergence. Alternatives (multi-parameter, weighted)
  were tried and abandoned.
- **P3→Markov:** Currently conditional on causal sufficiency. The three-part argument
  suggests the conditioning may be removable for agent-constructed strategies (where causal
  sufficiency is guaranteed by construction).

Tracking these separately from pure framework choices would help the presentation: it tells
the reader "we didn't just pick this; we tried everything else and it didn't work."

## What Neither Review Addressed

A few areas that deserve attention but weren't covered in depth:

- **The relationship to active inference / FEP.** The CIY segment notes a structural
  isomorphism with expected free energy. This connection deserves either a dedicated segment
  or explicit scoping: what does AAD add that active inference doesn't? The persistence
  condition has no FEP analog (FEP agents don't have a viability threshold). The adversarial
  dynamics have no FEP analog. These are genuine differentiators that should be stated.

- **The relationship to POMDP planning.** The strategy DAG is close to a POMDP policy tree.
  What does the AAD framing add? Mainly: the mismatch dynamics, the persistence condition,
  and the composition machinery. The strategy DAG alone is not novel; its embedding in the
  adaptive-systems framework is.

- **Computational complexity.** Several quantities (A_O, CIY, V_O, model sufficiency) are
  defined as expectations or suprema that are intractable to compute. The operationalization
  appendix addresses this for some quantities but not all. A systematic tractability analysis
  would help distinguish "theoretically defined but practically computable" from
  "theoretically defined and practically intractable."

- **The between-event dynamics.** The recursive-update segment defines g_M(M_τ) for
  between-event evolution but this is never developed. For agents that do significant
  computation between observations (LLMs generating tokens, humans thinking), the
  between-event dynamics may be where the real action is.
