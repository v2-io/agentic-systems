## Overall Impression

Section I is genuine mathematics. The Lyapunov machinery is applied correctly,
the gain-sector bridge does real eliminative work, and the mismatch
decomposition is an exact identity. The seven counterexample attacks on
recursive-update are what serious theoretical work looks like. The simulation
results shaped the theory (Model D/S split discovered empirically, then
derived) — the right relationship between simulation and analysis.

The epistemic discipline is the work's most distinctive quality. The
three-ring classification (inevitability core / canonical formulations /
empirical-heuristic) prevents the two most common theory-paper failure modes.
Every segment labels its own epistemic status with a precision I have not seen
elsewhere at this granularity. This earns credibility that polished but
epistemically undifferentiated presentation does not.

The synthesis — connecting Lyapunov stability, causal hierarchy, information
bottleneck, and gain principle under a single framework with the same
quantities (α, ρ, T, R) doing the same work across Kalman filters, RL bandits,
military OODA, and software development — is the contribution. The individual
pieces are known; the integration is novel.

The theory's external vulnerability is not internal inconsistency but
operationalization: can these quantities be measured in running agents? And can
the framework handle the dominant present-day agent class (LLMs), which
violates the scope restriction that enables Section II's cleanest results?

## Verified Issues

### From Codex audit (all 6 confirmed)

**C1. Causal sufficiency framed too optimistically in graph-structure-uniqueness.**

`graph-structure-uniqueness.md:95` says causal sufficiency is "a reasonable
assumption" for agent-constructed strategies. But `strategy-dag.md:78` calls
causal insufficiency "the dominant case" in complex settings, and
`strategy-dag.md:109` says the default in complex environments should be L1,
not L0.

The proof itself is fine — conditional on causal sufficiency, the CMC theorem
delivers the Markov factorization. The issue is the motivation: "reasonable
assumption" in the derivation segment vs. "dominant case [of violation]" in the
segment that uses the result. The text at line 95 does go on to discuss what
happens when the assumption fails ("the remedy is to add the missing
common-cause node"), but the initial framing understates how exceptional the
assumption is in the domains AAD most cares about.

**Fix:** Replace "reasonable assumption" with language acknowledging that causal
sufficiency is a modeling ideal that real strategies will typically violate, and
that the Correlation Hierarchy (L0/L1/L2) in #def-strategy-dag is the theory's
response. The proof remains conditional; the motivation should match the
theory's own assessment of the condition's typicality.


**C2. Observable intermediates stated as necessity; theory's own machinery
requires only identifiable aggregates.**

`graph-structure-uniqueness.md:55` says the strategy representation "must have
internal checkpoints." But `strategy-persistence-schema.md:47` explicitly
recovers persistence for an unobservable intermediate via plan-level tracking,
and `observability-dominance.md:49-51` says partial observability forces
plan-level aggregation rather than invalidating the strategy.

The "must" in P4 is too strong. Observable intermediates provide stronger
diagnostics and faster learning (the observability investment tradeoff in
`observability-dominance.md:53` quantifies this), but the theory works without
them at reduced diagnostic power. P4 as stated claims necessity; the theory
later demonstrates graceful degradation.

**Fix:** Reframe P4 from "must have" to "strongly benefits from" or split into
two claims: (a) observable intermediates enable localized revision (derived,
exact), and (b) without them, plan-level aggregation preserves persistence at
the cost of diagnostic resolution (derived, from the unobservable-intermediate
case in Props B.3/B.5).


**C3. Loop interventional access over-identifies acting-under-policy with clean
per-action do-identification.**

`loop-interventional-access.md:22` says "This is an intervention — the agent
varied its action and observed the result." The formal expression labels the
data "genuine intervention." Lines 23-24 immediately caveat this with four
complications (coverage, confounding, delay, partial observability), and the
epistemic status at line 34 distinguishes data availability from reasoning
capacity.

The body is appropriately cautious. The issue is the front-loaded framing:
"genuine intervention" in the formal expression vs. the regime classification in
`edge-update-causal-validity.md:26-34` which shows that clean extraction
depends on C1/C2/C3 conditions that are often only partially met. The data IS
interventional in character (the agent did act), but "genuine intervention"
suggests cleaner identification than the later machinery allows.

**Fix:** Adjust formal expression wording from "genuine intervention" to
something like "data generated under intervention" or "interventional in
character," and cross-reference the regime classification in
#scope-edge-update-causal-validity more prominently.


**C4. Orient cascade step 4 centers on δ_strategic despite δ_s being the
proved quantity.**

`orient-cascade.md:35` says step 4 uses δ_strategic as "the operational
diagnostic." The segment immediately notes the caveat: persistence is proved for
δ_s (plan-confidence error), not δ_strategic (per-edge calibration residual).
`credit-assignment-boundary.md:114` confirms AAD's formal guarantees require
only Level 0 plan-level tracking.

The caveat is present and clearly stated. The issue is that the step still
operationally points to the unproved quantity. From the theory's own formal
position, the default signal should be δ_s (credit-assignment-free, proved
persistent), with δ_strategic as the optional localization layer when credit
assignment is available.

**Fix:** Restructure step 4 to make δ_s the default operational signal. When
credit assignment is available (Level 1+), δ_strategic provides optional
per-edge localization. The hierarchy — persistence is credit-assignment-free;
calibration is not — should be the organizing principle.


**C5. Objective revision operationalized before Π/N_h/convention checks.**

`orient-cascade.md:38` says: "If δ_sat > 0 persists across Σ_t revisions —
revise O_t." But `satisfaction-gap.md:32-40` lists four possible causes of
positive δ_sat: infeasible goal, narrow Π, short N_h, wrong M_t. And
`value-object.md:88-90` notes that under C1 (the canonical default), δ_sat > 0
may mean "locally stuck" rather than "genuinely infeasible."

The cascade's step 5 jumps to O_t revision without requiring that the agent
first check whether Π expansion, N_h extension, or M_t improvement could
resolve the gap. The satisfaction-gap segment lists these alternatives, and the
value-object segment explains the convention dependence, but neither is built
into the cascade as a required escalation.

**Fix:** Add intermediate substeps to step 5: before O_t revision, check
whether (a) a richer policy class Π would close the gap (structural Σ_t
adaptation), (b) a longer horizon N_h reveals recovery paths (convention
escalation from C1 to C2), or (c) M_t correction changes the feasibility
assessment. Only if δ_sat persists across all of these should O_t revision
proceed. The v3 spike reportedly states this stronger formulation.


**C6. Composition-closure discussion slides past its own bridge-lemma caveat.**

`composition-closure.md:171` says: "an AAD agent that is structurally
persistent in its own right also persists as a faithful representation of its
constituents, because both require the same thing — contracting correction
dynamics under bounded perturbation."

But `composition-closure.md:161` explicitly says the bridge lemma needs "an
additional assumption beyond (A4), not a consequence of it." The formal
statement is precise; the discussion at line 171 treats A4 as sufficient when
the derivation says it is not. This is the classic pattern: the formal caveat
is carefully stated, but the surrounding prose recovers the stronger
interpretation the caveat was meant to prevent.

**Fix:** Qualify line 171 explicitly: "...because both require the same thing —
contracting correction dynamics under bounded perturbation — though the bridge
from (A4) to full-update-map contraction requires an additional assumption
verified for estimation-type agents but open in general (see Epistemic Status)."

**C10. Causal-insufficiency detection reads slightly strong.**

bias in the update rule. The "after n_k >> 1 for all k" condition addresses (a)
partially but doesn't verify convergence directly.

Would benefit from adding nonstationarity and incomplete convergence to the
explicit preconditions, framing the criterion as a strong indicator requiring
these preconditions rather than a near-mechanical diagnosis.

## Observations (not issues — patterns worth noting)

**O1. The "discussion recovers what the formal statement restricts" pattern.**

Items C3, C5, and C6 share a pattern: the formal expression or epistemic status
is carefully qualified, but the surrounding discussion, interpretation, or
operational prescription quietly adopts the stronger reading. This is natural
(the stronger version is easier to work with) but it's the theory's most
systematic presentational risk. The formal caveats earn trust; the discussion
sections risk spending it.

**O2. The gap between δ_s and δ_strategic runs deeper than a labeling issue.**

δ_s (plan-confidence error) is proved persistent, credit-assignment-free, and
computable from status propagation alone. δ_strategic (per-edge calibration
residual) is discussion-grade, requires credit assignment, and is inherently
slower (second-order inference over many edge traversals). The orient cascade's
step 4 uses δ_strategic; the persistence proof covers δ_s. This isn't just a
caveat to add — it reflects a structural gap between what the theory can
guarantee and what it operationally recommends. The hierarchy from
credit-assignment-boundary.md (Level 0: plan-level → Level 1: directional →
Level 2: approximate → Level 3: exact) is the right framework, but it should
organize the cascade, not just the credit-assignment discussion.

**O3. The theory's strength-in-depth is underrepresented by its stage labels.**

85 segments exist, but only 16 are claims-verified (plus 6 more that have been
promoted in frontmatter but not in the OUTLINE). The actual mathematical
content is substantially stronger than the stage distribution suggests. Many
draft segments contain precise derivations, verified instances, and honest
epistemic labels — they're "draft" because they haven't been through the formal
promotion gate, not because they're sketchy. The promotion bottleneck is
administrative, not theoretical.

## Narrative / Pedagogical Recommendations

*Source: Codex follow-up on preempting reader friction. Verified against
cited passages.*

The core pattern: **the repo has the right caveat, but it arrives one beat too
late.** The first strong formulation sticks; the later narrowing reads as
retreat rather than intended scope. The theory's substance is more careful than
its entry-point rhetoric.

### Specific fixes (each maps to a C-item above)

**N1. Strategy-layer exactness contract at Section II entry.** (→ C1)

Place a short framing statement at the top of Section II or strategy-dag.md:
"L0 gives the tractable proofs; L1 is the practical default in complex
domains." Currently the stronger L0-theorem story lands first in
graph-structure-uniqueness.md:93; the practical correction arrives later in
strategy-dag.md:78 and :109. Readers who stop at the derivation get the wrong
default.

**N2. Observable intermediates for diagnosis, not representation.** (→ C2)

graph-structure-uniqueness.md:55 says "must have internal checkpoints." Change
to "to support local edge revision" or "for localized strategic diagnosis." The
word "must" invites the objection that plan-level tracking in
strategy-persistence-schema.md:47 later answers. Preempt rather than repair.

**N3. "Intervention-produced data" not "this is an intervention."** (→ C3)

loop-interventional-access.md:20 should lead with the distinction between
"action-generated data" and "cleanly identified do-estimates." Currently the
careful caveat (line 24) arrives after the reader has already formed the
stronger interpretation. The distinction should be upfront.

**N4. Split orient-cascade step 4 into 4a/4b.** (→ C4)

4a: plan-level calibration (δ_s) — proved persistent, credit-assignment-free.
4b: edge-level localization (δ_strategic) — optional when observability permits,
requires credit assignment. This aligns the cascade's narrative with what the
theory has actually proved. Currently orient-cascade.md:35 foregrounds the
unproved quantity.

**N5. Bold convention warning before objective revision.** (→ C5)

Before any discussion of O_t revision, state: "Under C1 (the canonical
default), positive δ_sat means locally stuck, not globally infeasible." The
material exists in value-object.md:88 and satisfaction-gap.md:32, but a reader
reaching orient-cascade.md:38 can still infer that unmet goals naturally drive
objective revision unless they remember the convention hierarchy.

**N6. Carry the bridge-lemma condition through the prose.** (→ C6)

Repeat the section-level humility from OUTLINE.md:7 inside the
composition-closure segment itself: "closure defect is defined; bridge to
bounded trajectory error is conditional on contraction." The formal caveat at
composition-closure.md:161 is precise; the prose at :171 sounds more settled
than the condition allows. Readers benefit when the condition is carried all the
way through, not just stated once.

### General principle: "What this does not show" boxes

Section II would benefit from a recurring explicit-limitations framing. The raw
material already exists:

- Modular-only exactness: OUTLINE.md:64
- Convention-dependence: value-object.md:90
- L0-vs-L1 practical default: strategy-dag.md:109
- Credit-assignment boundary: credit-assignment-boundary.md:114
- Class 2 scope restriction: directed-separation.md (throughout)

These are exactly the things a skeptical reader will ask first. Surfacing them
at the point of first encounter — not after the formal machinery — prevents the
"retreat" impression.

**Meta-principle:** Move the caveat to the first paragraph. Make the
proved/default/practical distinction explicit before the formal machinery
starts. The theory is more careful than its rhetoric; close that gap and the
credibility follows.

## Suggested Priorities

Ranked by leverage: how much the theory's external credibility and internal
coherence improve per unit of effort.

### 1. Fix C1-C6 via the narrative recommendations (N1-N6)

The C-items and N-items are the same work. The narrative framing (N1-N6) is the
better approach — it preempts the reader's wrong impression rather than
repairing it after the fact. None requires new theory. Highest ratio of
credibility improvement to effort.

### 2. Update OUTLINE stage column (A1) and WORKBENCH staleness (A2)

The OUTLINE is the canonical presentation. Having 6 segments understated by one
stage level misrepresents the theory's maturity. Straightforward maintenance.

### 3. Promote architecturally critical Section II segments (A5)

directed-separation, strategy-dag, and graph-structure-uniqueness have received
substantial recent work (architectural classification, CMC proof, Correlation
Hierarchy). Promoting these from draft through deps-verified would reflect their
actual quality and unblock downstream promotions. This is verification effort,
not new theory.

### 4. Write the 7 logogenic segments (A3)

The coupled survival analysis spike provides clear content. The classification
(16/24 survive exactly, 5 approximately, 2 require modification) is the answer
to "does AAD apply to LLMs?" Getting this into published segments changes the
reception story from "doesn't apply to LLMs" to "here's exactly what applies
and what changes." This is the theory's most important external-credibility gap.

### 5. Write the 4 missing TST segments (A4)

Particularly developer-as-act-agent and code-quality-as-observation-
infrastructure. The operationalization story is AAD's defense against
"unmeasurable quantities." causal-discovery-from-git is important for honesty:
either write it or explicitly downgrade the git-as-interventional-data claim.

### 6. Restructure orient-cascade around the δ_s / δ_strategic hierarchy

This combines C4 and C5 into a coherent revision: make δ_s the default
operational signal, δ_strategic optional with credit assignment, and build
Π/N_h/convention escalation into step 5. Aligns the cascade with what the
theory actually proves.

### 7. Tighten composition bridge lemma characterization

The contraction assumption is verified for Kalman, open for general agents. The
most valuable next step is not proving the general case but characterizing the
boundary: what properties must a correction function have for sector-bounded
correction to imply full-update-map contraction? This converts an "open
assumption" into a checkable condition per domain.
